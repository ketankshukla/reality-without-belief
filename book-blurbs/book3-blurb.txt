What if almost everything you call "thinking" is actually a performance?

Rationalism has become ego armor. Opinions function as social currency. Outrage is not a response to wrongness -- it is a mechanism that keeps you locked in place. And beneath all of this elaborate performance, almost no one is actually thinking.

The Theater of Conviction exposes how belief operates at the cultural level -- through identity, outrage, online discourse, and the relentless performance of conviction. You'll discover why your strongest opinions may be your weakest thinking, and why the most certain voices in any room are usually the least examined.

Once you see the theater for what it is, you gain access to something extraordinary: the ability to act from genuine clarity rather than borrowed conviction.

Book 3 in the Reality Without Belief series.
