# Content Generation Guide

## Overview

This document provides detailed instructions for generating each type of content in a book project. Follow these guidelines to ensure consistency, quality, and publication-readiness.

---

## Part 1: Front Matter

### 1.1 Copyright Page

**Purpose:** Legal protection and publication information.

**Template:**
```markdown
# Copyright

© [Year] [Author Name]

All rights reserved. No part of this publication may be reproduced, distributed, or transmitted in any form or by any means, including photocopying, recording, or other electronic or mechanical methods, without the prior written permission of the publisher, except in the case of brief quotations embodied in critical reviews and certain other noncommercial uses permitted by copyright law.

**[Book Title]**
[Series Name] — Book [N]

First Edition: [Month Year]

ISBN: [Placeholder for ISBN]
ISBN (ebook): [Placeholder]

Published by [Publisher Name]
[Publisher Location]

[Optional: Website URL]

Cover design by [Designer/Metronagon Media]
Interior formatting by [Formatter]

Printed in [Country]
```

**Word Count:** 80-150 words

### 1.2 Dedication

**Purpose:** Personal acknowledgment that connects author to reader.

**Guidelines:**
- Keep it genuine and specific
- Match the book's tone
- Can be simple or elaborate depending on brief

**Examples by Style:**

**Simple:**
```markdown
# Dedication

To [Name] — who showed me what was possible.
```

**Elaborate:**
```markdown
# Dedication

To [Name],

Who never asked me to be anything other than what I am.
Who stood beside me when certainty crumbled.
Who is, in every way that matters, what is so.

This work exists because you do.
```

**Poetic:**
```markdown
# Dedication

For [Name] —
Not because you believed in this,
but because you never needed to.
```

**Word Count:** 20-150 words

---

### 1.3 Epigraph

**Purpose:** Set thematic tone with a relevant quote.

**Guidelines:**
- Choose quote that encapsulates the book's core thesis
- Attribution must be accurate
- Quote should be thought-provoking, not cliché

**Format:**
```markdown
# Epigraph

> "[Quote text]"
>
> — [Attribution, Source (Year)]
```

**Quote Selection Criteria:**
1. Directly relevant to book's central theme
2. From a respected or recognizable source
3. Not overused in the genre
4. Stands alone without context

**Suggested Sources by Genre:**

| Genre | Sources |
|-------|---------|
| Philosophy | Philosophers, scientists, thought leaders |
| Self-Help | Psychologists, authors, historical figures |
| Business | Entrepreneurs, economists, strategists |
| Fiction | Literary figures, poets, the work itself |

### 1.4 Preface

**Purpose:** Author's personal framing of why this book exists.

**Structure:**
1. **Opening hook** — Why this book, why now (1 paragraph)
2. **Author's journey** — Personal context for writing this (2-3 paragraphs)
3. **What this book is** — Clear statement of scope (1-2 paragraphs)
4. **What this book is NOT** — Manage expectations (1 paragraph)
5. **How to read** — Guidance for the reader (1 paragraph)
6. **Series context** (if applicable) — Where this fits (1 paragraph)

**Tone:** Personal, direct, inviting

**Word Count:** 500-1,000 words

**Example Opening:**
```markdown
# Preface

This book began as a question I could not stop asking: [central question].

For [time period], I [author's journey with the topic]. What I discovered changed everything — not because it provided easy answers, but because it dissolved the questions themselves.

[Continue with personal narrative leading to the book's creation...]
```

---

### 1.5 Introduction

**Purpose:** Comprehensive overview of the book's content and value proposition.

**Structure:**
1. **Hook** — Powerful opening that captures attention (1-2 paragraphs)
2. **The Problem** — What the reader is struggling with (2-3 paragraphs)
3. **The Promise** — What this book delivers (2-3 paragraphs)
4. **Chapter Preview** — What each chapter covers (varies by chapter count)
5. **The Journey Ahead** (for series) — Preview of future books
6. **Call to Engage** — Invitation to begin (1 paragraph)

**Tone:** Inspirational, confident, specific

**Word Count:** 1,500-3,000 words

**Chapter Preview Format:**
```markdown
## What You Will Discover

The [N] chapters that follow examine [overall theme]. You will discover:

**Chapter 1: [Title]** — [One powerful sentence describing the insight/transformation]

**Chapter 2: [Title]** — [One powerful sentence]

[Continue for all chapters...]

Each chapter is complete in itself, addressing a specific dimension of [topic]. Together, they form [description of the complete arc].
```

### 1.6 Prologue

**Purpose:** Narrative opening that creates emotional connection.

**Types:**

**Personal Story:**
- A defining moment in the author's life
- Shows, doesn't tell
- Ends with connection to book's themes

**Fictional Scene:**
- A scenario that illustrates the book's premise
- Vivid, immersive, emotionally engaging
- Reader sees themselves in the scene

**Historical Moment:**
- A significant event that sets context
- Demonstrates the stakes
- Connects past to present relevance

**Thought Experiment:**
- An imaginative scenario
- Challenges assumptions
- Opens the reader's mind

**Structure:**
1. **Scene setting** (1-2 paragraphs)
2. **Narrative unfolding** (3-5 paragraphs)
3. **Turning point/revelation** (1-2 paragraphs)
4. **Bridge to book** (1 paragraph)

**Word Count:** 800-1,500 words

## Part 2: Chapters

### 2.1 Chapter Structure

**Standard Chapter Flow:**

1. **Opening Hook** (100-200 words)
   - Provocative statement, question, or scene
   - Immediately engages the reader
   - Sets up the chapter's territory

2. **Core Content** (2,000-3,500 words)
   - Main argument or narrative
   - Examples, evidence, stories
   - Building complexity
   - Logical progression

3. **Key Insight/Turn** (200-400 words)
   - The "aha" moment
   - Where everything clicks
   - What makes this chapter valuable

4. **Practical Application** (200-500 words)
   - How to use this insight
   - What changes for the reader
   - Concrete next steps (if applicable)

5. **Bridge to Next** (50-100 words)
   - Hooks the next chapter
   - Creates momentum
   - Leaves reader wanting more

### 2.2 Writing Style Guidelines

**Based on Reality Without Belief standards:**

**DO:**
- Use em dashes (—) for rhetorical emphasis and rhythm
- Write in active voice
- Make every sentence earn its place
- Use short paragraphs for impact
- Build to powerful conclusions
- Frame everything in terms of what becomes POSSIBLE
- Be direct, confident, unapologetic

**DON'T:**
- Use hedging language ("maybe," "perhaps," "it could be")
- Include unnecessary warnings or disclaimers
- Apologize for challenging ideas
- Use filler phrases
- Over-explain or condescend
- Use passive voice unless intentional

**Tone Spectrum:**

```
AVOID                                     AIM FOR
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Preachy            →                      Inviting
Academic           →                      Accessible
Wishy-washy        →                      Direct
Negative framing   →                      Possibility framing
Comfort-seeking    →                      Truth-seeking
```

### 2.3 Chapter Naming Conventions

**File naming:**
```
chapter_[NN]_[slug].md
```

**Title formats:**

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| Statement | "Belief Is Not a Virtue" |
| Question | "Why Do We Cling to Certainty?" |
| Command | "Stop Believing, Start Seeing" |
| Paradox | "The Freedom of Having Nothing to Defend" |
| Number | "Five Ways Conviction Fails" |

### 2.4 Non-Fiction vs Fiction

**Non-Fiction Chapters:**
- Argument-driven
- Evidence and examples
- Conceptual frameworks
- Practical applications
- Build cumulative understanding

**Fiction Chapters:**
- Scene-driven
- Character and dialogue
- Show, don't tell
- Emotional arc
- Build narrative tension

## Part 3: Back Matter

### 3.1 Epilogue

**Purpose:** Close the journey and point forward.

**Structure:**

1. **Reflection** — What the reader has traveled through (2-3 paragraphs)
2. **Synthesis** — How it all connects (2-3 paragraphs)
3. **What Remains** — The lasting change/insight (2-3 paragraphs)
4. **Coming Next** (if series) — Preview of next book (3-5 paragraphs)
5. **Final Words** — Powerful closing (1-2 paragraphs)

**Word Count:** 800-2,000 words

**Coming Next Section Format:**
```markdown
## Coming Next: [Next Book Title]

The journey continues in [Book Title] — [Subtitle].

Having discovered [what this book covered], the next territory awaits: [next book's territory].

In the [N] chapters ahead, you will discover:

- [Provocative preview of Chapter 1]
- [Provocative preview of Chapter 2]
- [Continue for key chapters...]

[Compelling hook paragraph that creates urgency to continue]
```

---

### 3.2 Acknowledgments

**Purpose:** Thank those who made the book possible.

**Structure:**
1. Primary influences/inspirations
2. Personal supporters (family, friends)
3. Professional contributors (editors, designers)
4. Readers and community
5. Brief closing

**Tone:** Warm, genuine, specific

**Word Count:** 300-600 words

---

### 3.3 About the Author

**Purpose:** Establish credibility and connection.

**Structure:**
1. Credentials and expertise (1-2 sentences)
2. Personal background (1-2 sentences)
3. Why they write (1-2 sentences)
4. Other works (1 sentence)
5. Where to find them (1 sentence)

**Word Count:** 150-400 words

**Format:**
```markdown
# About the Author

[Author Name] is [credentials and primary identity].

[Personal background that establishes credibility for this book's topic.]

[Why they write / their mission.]

[Previous works, if any.]

Connect with [Author] at [website] or on [social media platforms].
```

---

### 3.4 Also By

**Purpose:** Cross-sell other works.

**Format:**
```markdown
# Also by [Author Name]

## [Series Name]

- **Book 1: [Title]** — [Subtitle]
- **Book 2: [Title]** — [Subtitle]
- **Book 3: [Title]** — [Subtitle]

## Other Works

- **[Title]** — [Brief description]
- **[Title]** — [Brief description]

Available wherever books are sold.
```

### 3.5 Call to Action

**Purpose:** Convert readers to engaged fans.

**Elements (choose relevant):**
- Review request
- Newsletter signup
- Website visit
- Social media follow
- Next book pre-order

**Example:**
```markdown
# A Request

If this book has shifted something for you — if you see differently than you did before — I have a favor to ask.

**Leave a review.** Not for me, but for the next reader who is standing where you stood. Your honest words might be the reason they take this journey.

[Link to review page]

And if you want to continue the conversation, join me at [website] where [what they'll find].

Thank you for reading. Thank you for seeing.

— [Author Name]
```

## Part 4: Summaries & Marketing Content

### 4.1 Chapter Summaries

**Purpose:** 3-minute verbal delivery summaries for podcasts, videos, or quick reference.

**Structure:**
```markdown
# Chapter [N] Summary: [Title]

## Core Argument

[One paragraph — 3-4 sentences stating the main thesis clearly and powerfully]

## Key Points

- **[Point 1 Title]** — [1-2 sentence explanation]
- **[Point 2 Title]** — [1-2 sentence explanation]
- **[Point 3 Title]** — [1-2 sentence explanation]
- **[Point 4 Title]** — [1-2 sentence explanation]

## One-Line Takeaway

[Single powerful sentence that captures the chapter's essence]
```

**Word Count:** 200-350 words

---

### 4.2 Book Summary (Video Script)

**Purpose:** 5-minute inspirational script for AI-generated book trailers.

**Structure:**
1. **Hook** (30 seconds) — Grab attention immediately
2. **Problem** (45 seconds) — What the reader struggles with
3. **Journey** (2 minutes) — What the book covers
4. **Transformation** (1 minute) — What changes for the reader
5. **Call to Action** (45 seconds) — "Buy the book now"

**Tone:** Extremely inspirational, motivating, cinematic

**Format:**
- No scene markers or metadata
- Flowing paragraphs designed for narration
- Short sentences for impact
- Rhetorical questions for engagement
- Ends with clear CTA

**Word Count:** 700-900 words (5 minutes at ~150 WPM)

---

### 4.3 Book Description (Amazon)

**Purpose:** Convert browsers to buyers.

**Format:** HTML for Amazon KDP

**Structure:**
```html
<b>Hook — one powerful line that stops the scroll</b>

<p>Opening paragraph establishing the problem/premise.</p>

<p><i>[Book Title]</i> is [positioning statement and promise].</p>

<p><b>In these pages, you will discover:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>[Benefit/insight 1 — specific and compelling]</li>
<li>[Benefit/insight 2]</li>
<li>[Benefit/insight 3]</li>
<li>[Benefit/insight 4]</li>
<li>[Benefit/insight 5]</li>
</ul>

<p>This book is for [target reader description].</p>

<p><i>[Closing hook — creates urgency or FOMO]</i></p>

<p><b>[Series/author positioning]</b></p>
```

**Word Count:** 200-400 words

**Keywords:** Integrate naturally throughout (for Amazon SEO)

## Part 5: Quality Standards

### Consistency Checks

Before completing any content:

- [ ] Tone matches brief specifications throughout
- [ ] Style guidelines followed (em dashes, active voice, etc.)
- [ ] Chapter hooks connect properly
- [ ] Cross-references accurate (if series)
- [ ] Names, titles, and terms consistent
- [ ] No placeholder text remaining
- [ ] Word counts within target ranges

### Final Review Questions

1. Does this content fulfill the brief's requirements?
2. Would the target reader find this valuable?
3. Is every sentence necessary?
4. Does it build to a satisfying conclusion?
5. Does it leave the reader wanting more?
