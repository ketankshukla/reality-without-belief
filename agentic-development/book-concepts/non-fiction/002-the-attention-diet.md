# The Attention Diet

## Genre
Self-Development / Digital Wellness / Productivity

## Concept

You've optimized your food. You've optimized your fitness. Now it's time to optimize what you feed your mind. This book introduces the concept of an "attention diet" — a systematic approach to consuming information the way nutritionists approach consuming food.

Just as we've learned that not all calories are equal, not all information is equal. Some inputs nourish your thinking, creativity, and wellbeing. Others are mental junk food — immediately satisfying but ultimately depleting. And just like physical diet, most people have no idea how much mental junk they're consuming because they've never tracked it.

This book provides a practical framework for auditing your information intake, identifying what's nourishing vs. depleting, and designing a sustainable attention diet that supports the life and mind you actually want. It's not about digital detox or going offline — it's about intentional consumption.

## Key Themes
- Information as food for the mind
- The hidden costs of information junk
- Attention as finite, depletable resource
- Quality vs. quantity of mental input
- Designing your information environment
- Sustainable digital wellness (not extreme abstinence)

## Tone
Practical, research-backed, non-preachy. The author has struggled with this too and isn't lecturing from a mountaintop.

## Unique Angle
Instead of telling you to "go offline," this book gives you a systematic framework for being online intentionally — treating attention like the resource it is.

## Target Audience
Knowledge workers, creators, anyone who feels mentally depleted by their information environment. Adults 25-50 who consume a lot of content and feel it affecting them.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (10 chapters)
1. The Mental Obesity Epidemic — state of attention in modern life
2. Information Nutrition — not all inputs are equal
3. The Audit — tracking your actual consumption for one week
4. Junk Identification — recognizing low-quality inputs
5. Nourishing Sources — what actually feeds good thinking
6. The Elimination Phase — clearing the obvious toxins
7. Designing Your Environment — making good choices easy
8. Meal Planning for the Mind — structuring your information day
9. Cheat Days and Sustainability — avoiding puritanical failure
10. The Attention-Rich Life — what becomes possible
