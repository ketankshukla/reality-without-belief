# The Mind-Body Disconnect

## Genre
Health / Psychology / Self-Development

## Concept

Modern life has created an unprecedented separation between mind and body. We live from the neck up, treating our bodies as vehicles for our brains, ignoring physical signals until they become crises. This disconnection underlies much of our chronic stress, mysterious symptoms, and general feeling of being unwell despite no diagnosis.

This book explores how the mind-body split developed, what it costs us, and practical ways to reconnect. It bridges neuroscience, somatic therapy, and practical exercises to help readers inhabit their bodies again.

The key insight: your body isn't just where you keep your brain. It's a source of intelligence, a regulator of emotion, and a partner in wellbeing that most people have stopped consulting.

## Key Themes
- How we became disembodied
- Interoception and body awareness
- The body as emotional regulator
- Physical symptoms of psychological problems
- Somatic practices for reconnection
- Living as embodied beings

## Tone
Integrative, scientifically grounded, gently encouraging. The author has been through this disconnection.

## Unique Angle
Not yoga promotion or alternative medicine, but a science-based case for paying attention to the body.

## Target Audience
Knowledge workers who live in their heads. People with chronic tension or unexplained symptoms. Anyone who feels disconnected from their physical self.
