# The Self-Trust Project

## Genre
Psychology / Self-Development / Personal Growth

## Concept

Most self-help focuses on self-improvement: becoming better, smarter, more productive. This book focuses on something more fundamental: self-trust. The ability to rely on yourself, to know you'll follow through on commitments to yourself, to believe your own judgments.

Self-trust is built the same way trust with others is built: through reliability over time. And it's destroyed the same way: through broken promises, inconsistency, and betrayal. Every time you don't do what you said you'd do, you erode trust with yourself.

This book provides a framework for rebuilding self-trust: making smaller promises, keeping them religiously, and slowly expanding the scope of what you can trust yourself to do.

## Key Themes
- Self-trust as foundation of self-esteem
- How self-trust is built (reliability over time)
- How self-trust is destroyed (broken self-promises)
- Rebuilding through small commitments
- The relationship between self-trust and confidence
- Living as someone you can rely on

## Tone
Honest, encouraging, structured. The author has rebuilt self-trust and shares the process.

## Unique Angle
Not motivation or willpower but trustworthiness to yourself — reframing personal development.

## Target Audience
People who don't trust themselves to follow through. Those with a history of self-broken promises. Anyone whose self-esteem has been eroded by their own inconsistency.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (10 chapters)
1. The Trust Crisis — why you don't trust yourself
2. How Trust Works — the mechanics
3. How Trust Breaks — self-betrayal patterns
4. The Small Promise — starting to rebuild
5. The Keeping — following through reliably
6. The Expansion — building on small successes
7. Self-Trust and Self-Esteem — the connection
8. Self-Trust and Decisions — trusting your judgment
9. Self-Trust and Others — how it affects relationships
10. The Reliable Self — living as someone you can trust
