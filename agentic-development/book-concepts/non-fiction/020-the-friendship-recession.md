# The Friendship Recession

## Genre
Sociology / Self-Development / Relationships

## Concept

Adults are experiencing a friendship crisis. Studies show that the average number of close friends has plummeted over the past three decades. People are lonelier than ever, not because they don't want friends, but because adult life seems designed to prevent friendship formation.

This book explores why adult friendship has become so difficult — the mobility, the busyness, the awkwardness of friend-making after school — and provides a practical framework for building and maintaining adult friendships in a world that doesn't support them.

The key insight: friendship doesn't happen naturally in adulthood. You have to engineer it, with the same intentionality you'd bring to your career or health.

## Key Themes
- The structural barriers to adult friendship
- Why we're lonelier than ever
- Friend-making as adult skill
- Maintaining friendships across distance and time
- Different types of adult friendships
- Building community intentionally

## Tone
Honest about the problem, practical about solutions. The author has struggled with this and isn't preaching from a social mountaintop.

## Unique Angle
Not cheerleading about friendship's importance (we know), but practical infrastructure for making it happen.

## Target Audience
Adults who've noticed their friendships declining. People who moved cities and lost their social network. Anyone who's lonely but doesn't know how to fix it.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (10 chapters)
1. The Friendship Recession — data on the decline
2. Why It's So Hard — structural barriers to adult friendship
3. The Friend-Making Skills — what you weren't taught
4. Finding Potential Friends — where to look
5. The Approach — how to initiate
6. The Deepening — from acquaintance to friend
7. Maintenance — keeping friendships alive
8. Different Friend Types — not everyone needs to be a best friend
9. Building Community — beyond individual friendships
10. The Connected Life — what changes when you're not lonely
