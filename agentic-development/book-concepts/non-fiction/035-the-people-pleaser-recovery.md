# The People Pleaser Recovery

## Genre
Psychology / Self-Development / Relationships

## Concept

People pleasing isn't kindness — it's a survival strategy that stopped working. If you compulsively say yes, struggle to state preferences, feel responsible for others' emotions, and experience resentment after bending over backward, this book is your recovery guide.

Drawing on attachment theory, codependency research, and practical psychology, this book explains where people pleasing comes from, what it costs, and how to develop healthy self-advocacy while maintaining genuine kindness.

The goal isn't to become selfish — it's to become honest. To say yes when you mean yes and no when you mean no. To be kind without disappearing.

## Key Themes
- Origins of people pleasing (usually childhood)
- The cost of chronic accommodation
- The difference between kindness and pleasing
- Setting boundaries without guilt
- Learning to tolerate others' disappointment
- Authentic relating vs. performance

## Tone
Validating, practical, gently challenging. The author has recovered from people pleasing.

## Unique Angle
Not just "set boundaries" advice but deep understanding of why it's so hard and how to actually do it.

## Target Audience
Chronic accommodators, those with codependent tendencies, anyone who's lost themselves in trying to make others happy.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (11 chapters)
1. The Pleasing Pattern — what it looks like
2. Origins — where it came from
3. The Cost — what people pleasing costs you
4. Kindness vs. Pleasing — an important distinction
5. The Resentment Signal — your body's rebellion
6. Boundary Basics — what boundaries actually are
7. The Guilt — handling the discomfort
8. Tolerating Disappointment — others' reactions
9. The No Practice — learning to refuse
10. Authentic Connection — relating without pleasing
11. The Recovered Life — kindness without disappearing
