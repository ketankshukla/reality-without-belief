# The Wisdom of Enough

## Genre
Philosophy / Self-Development / Cultural Criticism

## Concept

Modern life is organized around "more" — more money, more success, more possessions, more experiences, more optimization. But research consistently shows that beyond a certain point, more doesn't make you happier. This book explores the wisdom of "enough" — how to define it, how to recognize when you've reached it, and how to stop there.

"Enough" isn't failure to achieve more. It's wisdom about what actually serves your wellbeing. This book provides frameworks for identifying your personal "enough" in money, career, possessions, relationships, and achievement — and the discipline to stop pursuing more when you've arrived.

The paradox: people who define and pursue "enough" often end up happier than those who pursue "more" indefinitely.

## Key Themes
- The "more" trap and its costs
- Defining personal enough
- The hedonic treadmill
- Enough in different domains
- The discipline of stopping
- Contentment as achievement

## Tone
Philosophical but practical, countercultural but accessible. The author has found their enough and shares the path.

## Unique Angle
Not minimalism (less is more) but sufficiency (enough is enough) — a positive definition rather than negation.

## Target Audience
Successful people who still feel unsatisfied. Those sensing that the pursuit is the problem. Readers interested in contentment over achievement.
