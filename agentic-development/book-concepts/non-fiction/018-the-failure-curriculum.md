# The Failure Curriculum

## Genre
Self-Development / Education / Business

## Concept

Schools teach success. This book teaches failure — specifically, how to fail well, fail forward, and extract maximum learning from every setback. Because in real life, you'll fail far more than you'll succeed, and no one taught you how.

Drawing on research, historical examples, and practical frameworks, this book provides a systematic approach to failure. It covers how to evaluate failures (some are useful, some aren't), how to extract lessons without over-learning, how to maintain confidence while acknowledging mistakes, and how to build a failure practice that accelerates growth.

The paradox: people who fail well succeed more than people who avoid failure entirely.

## Key Themes
- Failure as curriculum, not punishment
- Types of failure and what each teaches
- Extracting lessons without over-correction
- Maintaining confidence through failure
- Creating safe failure environments
- The failure-to-success pipeline

## Tone
Encouraging, evidence-based, practical. The author has failed plenty and is better for it.

## Unique Angle
Not "failure is good" cheerleading, but a systematic approach to making failure useful.

## Target Audience
Entrepreneurs and creators who fail regularly. Perfectionists paralyzed by fear of failure. Anyone who wants to take more risks with less emotional damage.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (10 chapters)
1. The Failure Gap — what you weren't taught
2. Types of Failure — categorizing setbacks
3. The Autopsy — how to analyze a failure
4. Lesson Extraction — what to actually learn
5. The Over-Correction Trap — not learning the wrong things
6. Confidence Maintenance — failing without collapsing
7. Intelligent Risk — creating safe failure zones
8. Failure Practice — deliberately practicing setbacks
9. Organizational Failure — team and company failures
10. The Failure-Fluent Life — becoming comfortable with setbacks
