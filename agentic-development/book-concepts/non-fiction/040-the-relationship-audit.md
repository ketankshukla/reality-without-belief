# The Relationship Audit

## Genre
Relationships / Self-Development / Psychology

## Concept

Your relationships are your life's operating system — they affect everything. Yet most people have never systematically evaluated their relationship portfolio: who's in it, what each relationship provides, what it costs, and whether the overall mix is healthy.

This book provides a framework for auditing your relationships: evaluating which ones nourish you, which drain you, which need repair, and which might need release. It's not about being ruthless or transactional — it's about being intentional with your most important resource.

The relationship audit isn't a one-time event but an ongoing practice of tending your relational garden: nurturing what's valuable, pruning what's overgrown, and sometimes pulling weeds.

## Key Themes
- Relationships as life's foundation
- The audit framework (assess, evaluate, decide)
- Nourishing vs. draining relationships
- Repair vs. release decisions
- Building an intentional relationship portfolio
- Ongoing relationship maintenance

## Tone
Honest but compassionate, practical, non-judgmental. The author values relationships and wants to help you tend yours.

## Unique Angle
Not about any single relationship but about your entire relationship ecosystem — portfolio thinking.

## Target Audience
Adults who feel their relationships are happening to them. Those at life transitions reconsidering their social world. Anyone who suspects some relationships aren't serving them.
