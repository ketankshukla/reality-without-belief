# The Meaning Crisis

## Genre
Philosophy / Psychology / Cultural Criticism

## Concept

We're living through a meaning crisis. The old sources of meaning — religion, tradition, community, stable careers — have eroded without clear replacements. The result is a generation experiencing material abundance and spiritual poverty, asking "is this all there is?" while having everything they were promised.

This book explores the history of how we got here, the psychology of meaning-making, and practical frameworks for constructing personal meaning in an age that doesn't provide it automatically. It's philosophy for people who don't have time for philosophy but desperately need it.

The thesis: meaning isn't found, it's made. And making it is a skill that can be learned.

## Key Themes
- The erosion of traditional meaning sources
- Why success doesn't feel meaningful
- The psychology of meaning-making
- Building personal meaning frameworks
- Purpose, values, and direction
- Living meaningfully without cosmic answers

## Tone
Philosophically serious but accessible, personally searching. The author is working through this too.

## Unique Angle
Not spiritual answers or positive thinking, but practical philosophy for meaning construction.

## Target Audience
Successful people who feel empty. Secular seekers. Anyone asking "what's the point?" after achieving goals.
