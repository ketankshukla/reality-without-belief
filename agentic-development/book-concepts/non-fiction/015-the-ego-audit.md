# The Ego Audit

## Genre
Psychology / Self-Development / Philosophy

## Concept

Your ego isn't your enemy — but it might be running parts of your life without your consent. This book introduces the concept of an "ego audit": a systematic examination of where your ego is serving you and where it's sabotaging you.

Most ego advice falls into two camps: "destroy your ego" (impossible and undesirable) or "feed your ego" (leads to narcissism). This book offers a third path: understand your ego's patterns, redirect its energy where useful, and create space around it where it causes harm.

Through assessment tools, reflection exercises, and practical techniques, you'll learn to identify your ego's signature moves, recognize when it's driving your behavior, and develop the awareness to choose whether to follow it.

## Key Themes
- Ego as servant not master
- Mapping your ego's patterns
- Where ego helps vs. harms
- Creating space around reactivity
- Redirecting ego energy productively
- The balanced relationship with self

## Tone
Honest, introspective, non-judgmental. The author has their own ego struggles and shares them.

## Unique Angle
Neither ego destruction nor ego celebration — practical ego management.

## Target Audience
Adults interested in self-awareness. Leaders wanting to check their blind spots. Anyone who's noticed their ego causing problems but doesn't want to "kill" it.
