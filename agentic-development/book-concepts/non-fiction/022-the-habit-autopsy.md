# The Habit Autopsy

## Genre
Self-Development / Psychology / Productivity

## Concept

You've read the habit books. You know about cue-routine-reward. You've tried stacking, tracking, and accountability. And your habits still don't stick. This book examines why — by conducting an autopsy on dead habits.

Most habit advice assumes the problem is knowledge or motivation. But the real killers of habits are often invisible: identity conflicts, environment mismatches, willpower timing, social context, and emotional triggers. This book helps you diagnose why your specific habits fail by examining the specific ways they die.

Through a systematic autopsy framework, you'll identify your personal habit failure patterns and design interventions that address the actual cause of death, not just the symptoms.

## Key Themes
- Why habits die (beyond "no willpower")
- Personal failure pattern identification
- The autopsy framework for dead habits
- Identity-habit conflicts
- Environment-behavior mismatches
- Designing for your specific failure modes

## Tone
Diagnostic, curious, non-judgmental. The author has killed many habits and learned to examine the bodies.

## Unique Angle
Not how to build habits but why yours specifically die — personalized diagnosis.

## Target Audience
People who've tried habit books and failed. Those with a graveyard of abandoned habits. Anyone who wants to understand their specific failure patterns.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (10 chapters)
1. The Habit Graveyard — all the habits that died
2. The Autopsy Framework — how to examine a dead habit
3. Death by Identity — when habits conflict with self-image
4. Death by Environment — context that kills
5. Death by Timing — willpower and energy mismatches
6. Death by Social Context — relationships that undermine
7. Death by Emotion — triggered failures
8. Your Pattern — identifying your signature failure mode
9. The Resurrection — designing for your specific failures
10. The Living Habit — what sustainable habits look like
