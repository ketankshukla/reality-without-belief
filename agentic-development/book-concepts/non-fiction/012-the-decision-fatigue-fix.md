# The Decision Fatigue Fix

## Genre
Productivity / Psychology / Self-Development

## Concept

Every day, you make thousands of decisions — from what to wear to what to eat to whether to reply to that email. Each decision depletes a finite resource: your decision-making capacity. By afternoon, you're making worse choices, defaulting to the path of least resistance, and wondering why you have no willpower.

This book explains the science of decision fatigue and provides a systematic approach to reclaiming your mental energy. The key insight: it's not about making better decisions — it's about making fewer decisions, so the ones that matter get your best thinking.

Through habit design, environment optimization, and strategic automation, you'll learn to eliminate trivial choices, batch related decisions, and protect your cognitive resources for what actually deserves them.

## Key Themes

## Tone
Research-backed, practical, immediately applicable. The author has designed their own life this way.

## Unique Angle
Unlike productivity books that help you do more, this helps you decide less — freeing energy for what matters.

## Target Audience
Busy professionals, parents, entrepreneurs — anyone overwhelmed by constant small decisions. Adults who feel drained by the end of every day.
