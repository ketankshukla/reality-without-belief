# The Body Keeps the Stress

## Genre
Health / Psychology / Self-Development

## Concept

Chronic stress isn't just in your head — it's in your shoulders, your jaw, your gut, your breath. This book explores the physical manifestations of psychological stress, how the body stores tension that the mind refuses to process, and practical somatic techniques for releasing what talk therapy alone can't reach.

Drawing on the latest research in psychosomatic medicine, polyvagal theory, and bodywork traditions, this book provides a map of where stress lives in the body and a toolkit for releasing it. It's for anyone who's "done the therapy" but still carries tension, who intellectually understands their stress but physically can't let go.

The approach is practical and non-woo: grounded in science, actionable in daily life, and respectful of both psychological and physical dimensions of human experience.

## Key Themes
- The body as stress storage system
- Somatic manifestations of psychological states
- Polyvagal theory made practical
- Release techniques from various traditions
- The limits of cognitive approaches
- Integrating body and mind work

## Tone
Warm, embodied, practically focused. The author has been through this and speaks from experience as well as research.

## Unique Angle
Unlike purely psychological books or purely bodywork books, this integrates both — acknowledging that stress is neither "just mental" nor "just physical."

## Target Audience
Adults carrying chronic tension, anxiety sufferers, people who've done therapy but still feel stuck in their bodies. Those interested in somatic approaches.
