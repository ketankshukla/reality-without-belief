# The Social Media Detox

## Genre
Digital Wellness / Self-Development / Cultural Criticism

## Concept

This isn't another book telling you social media is bad. You already know that. This is a practical guide for actually reducing or eliminating it — including the psychological, social, and practical challenges that make quitting so hard.

Social media isn't just a habit — it's an infrastructure. It's how you stay informed, maintain relationships, build professional presence, and fill idle moments. Quitting requires replacing all of these functions, not just deleting apps.

This book provides a structured detox process: understanding your specific usage patterns, building replacement structures, handling the social pressure to stay, and designing a post-social-media life that's actually richer.

## Key Themes
- Social media as infrastructure, not just habit
- Understanding your specific usage patterns
- Building replacement systems
- The social pressure to stay connected
- What life looks like after social media
- Selective use vs. complete elimination

## Tone
Practical, non-judgmental, structured. The author has done this and knows it's hard.

## Unique Angle
Not moralizing about social media but practical process for changing your relationship with it.

## Target Audience
People who've tried to quit and failed. Those who know social media is harming them but don't know how to stop. Anyone considering a different relationship with platforms.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (10 chapters)
1. The Hook — why social media is hard to quit
2. Your Pattern — understanding your specific usage
3. The Functions — what social media provides you
4. Replacement Structures — alternatives for each function
5. The Detox Process — a structured reduction plan
6. Social Pressure — handling others' expectations
7. The Void — what to do with empty time
8. Professional Considerations — career without platforms
9. Selective Return — if you choose partial use
10. The Post-Social Life — what opens up
