# The Divorce Architect

## Genre
Literary Fiction / Domestic Drama / Dark Comedy

## Concept

Nadia Volkov is the most sought-after divorce attorney in Manhattan — not because she wins cases, but because she prevents them. Her specialty is the "elegant dissolution": helping couples separate their lives so cleanly, so fairly, so thoughtfully that they often end up better friends than they were spouses. She's built a reputation on the philosophy that divorce doesn't have to destroy — it can be a masterpiece of mutual release.

But Nadia has a secret: she has never been in love. Not once. She has dated, lived with partners, even been engaged twice — but she has never felt what her clients describe when they weep in her office about what they're losing. She built her career studying love from the outside, the way an architect studies buildings she'll never live in.

Everything changes when she takes on her most challenging case: a celebrity couple whose public image depends on staying together, but who privately have become toxic. As Nadia works to untangle their lives, she finds herself falling for the husband — a man she's ethically forbidden to pursue, a man still in love with his wife, a man who makes her feel something she's spent forty-three years convinced she was incapable of feeling. Now the divorce architect must face her own construction: the carefully built life that protected her from love is exactly what's preventing her from experiencing it.

## Key Themes
- The architecture of relationships and their dissolution
- Emotional avoidance disguised as competence
- What it means to truly feel versus observe feeling
- Ethics, boundaries, and forbidden desires
- The stories we tell ourselves about who we are
- Whether it's ever too late to become vulnerable

## Tone
Sophisticated, wry, emotionally intelligent. The prose should feel urbane and controlled — like Nadia herself — until it cracks. Sharp dialogue, keen observations of wealth and relationship dynamics.

## Unique Angle
Most divorce stories focus on the couple. This one focuses on the person who guides them through it — and what happens when she can no longer remain professionally detached.

## Target Audience
Readers of literary fiction with relationship focus. Fans of Sally Rooney, Curtis Sittenfeld, and Meg Wolitzer. Women 30-60 interested in smart emotional fiction. Book clubs.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (12 chapters)
1. The elegant dissolution — Nadia's philosophy in action
2. The celebrity case — Mira and James Thornton
3. The architect's blueprints — Nadia's careful life
4. First meeting — James, and something unexpected
5. The toxicity beneath — discovering the marriage's truth
6. Historical patterns — Nadia's romantic past, or lack thereof
7. Dangerous proximity — working closely with James
8. The confession — James admits his feelings
9. The boundary — Nadia's ethical stand (and internal war)
10. Demolition — Nadia's constructed life begins cracking
11. The choice — professional ruin or personal truth
12. Reconstruction — aftermath and new architecture

## Visual Identity Suggestions
- Color palette: Sophisticated neutrals, champagne gold, architectural grays, romantic deep red
- Cover concept: A blueprint dissolving into rose petals, or two wedding rings being carefully separated by elegant hands
- Typography: Modern, clean serif — sophisticated and slightly cold
