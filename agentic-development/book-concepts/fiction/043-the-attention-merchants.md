# The Attention Merchants

## Genre
Satire / Dystopian Fiction / Corporate Thriller

## Concept

In 2039, attention has become the world's most valuable currency — literally. A neural interface measures exactly how much focus you give to anything, and advertisers pay you directly for your attention. Look at a billboard for three seconds: $0.02. Watch a full commercial with verified engagement: $0.75. Most people earn their living simply by paying attention to things corporations want them to see.

Sofia Reyes was an "attention farmer" — someone who maximized their earnings by consuming as much sponsored content as possible. She was good at it, earning six figures just by looking at the right things. Then she noticed her attention was degrading. She couldn't focus on anything that wasn't paying her. Her relationships suffered because unsponsored conversations felt worthless. She couldn't read books because no one was paying for that attention.

When Sofia tries to quit the attention economy, she discovers it's nearly impossible. The entire infrastructure of life — housing, food, transportation — now requires attention payments. And the corporations that profit from attention have become so powerful that "attention theft" — consuming content without verified engagement — has become a crime. Sofia must find a way to reclaim her mind while the most powerful industry in history fights to keep it.

## Key Themes
- The commodification of human attention
- What happens when you can't afford to daydream
- Corporate capture of consciousness
- The loss of undirected thought
- Addiction by design
- Reclaiming mental sovereignty

## Tone
Sharp satire that becomes increasingly urgent. The prose should feel overstimulating, like the world it depicts.

## Unique Angle
This isn't about technology being imposed — everyone chose this because it paid well. The horror is consensual.

## Target Audience
Readers of corporate dystopia and satire. Fans of Dave Eggers, Black Mirror, and anyone exhausted by the attention economy.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (13 chapters)
1. The farm — Sofia's optimized attention life
2. The degradation — noticing cognitive decline
3. The economy — how attention currency works
4. The attempt — trying to unplug
5. The withdrawal — what happens without stimulation
6. The infrastructure — how unplugging becomes impossible
7. The underground — finding others who resist
8. The crime — attention theft laws
9. The investigation — corporations notice Sofia
10. The choice — comply or fight
11. The exposure — revealing system's true costs
12. The revolution — attention strike
13. The free mind — aftermath and partial victory

## Visual Identity Suggestions
- Color palette: Screen glare blues, notification reds, algorithmic greens, rare analog browns
- Cover concept: An eye with advertisements reflected in it, or a brain with price tags attached
- Typography: Notification-style fonts, branded aesthetics
