# The Memory Merchant

## Genre
Psychological Thriller / Speculative Fiction

## Concept

In a near-future where memories can be extracted, edited, and sold on a black market, a disgraced neuroscientist named Elena Vance discovers that someone has been stealing her memories without her knowledge — not random ones, but specifically the memories of her daughter who died three years ago. As she investigates, she uncovers a shadowy network of "memory merchants" who harvest grief, trauma, and loss from unwitting victims and sell these intense emotional experiences to wealthy clients seeking to feel something real in their anesthetized lives.

The deeper Elena digs, the more she realizes her daughter's death may not have been an accident — and the memories being stolen contain evidence of a murder she witnessed but was made to forget. Now she must navigate a world where her own mind is unreliable, where the people hunting her can literally erase themselves from her memory, and where the only way to find the truth is to recover memories that someone has gone to extraordinary lengths to destroy.

## Key Themes
- The commodification of human emotion and experience
- Grief as a resource to be exploited
- The unreliability of memory and identity
- Corporate ethics in neurotechnology
- A mother's love transcending manipulation

## Tone
Dark, paranoid, emotionally devastating. The prose should feel disorienting at times, reflecting Elena's fractured memory state. Moments of profound tenderness cut through the thriller elements.

## Unique Angle
Unlike typical memory-manipulation stories, this one focuses on grief specifically — asking what happens when your pain becomes someone else's entertainment, and whether you can trust your own mourning when the memories themselves may have been altered.

## Target Audience
Readers of Blake Crouch, Kazuo Ishiguro's "Never Let Me Go," and fans of "Black Mirror." Adults 25-55 who enjoy speculative fiction with emotional depth.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (12-14 chapters)
1. The first gap — Elena notices missing time
2. The memory clinic — seeking diagnosis
3. The daughter who keeps fading — memories degrading
4. First contact with the underground
5. The merchants and their clients
6. What grief is worth — the market revealed
7. The night of the accident — fragmentary recovery
8. Hunting the hunter — Elena fights back
9. The architect of forgetting — antagonist revealed
10. Memory as weapon — Elena's counterattack
11. The truth she forgot — the murder witnessed
12. Reclamation — final confrontation
13. What remains — aftermath and ambiguous closure

## Visual Identity Suggestions
- Color palette: Deep blues, silver, clinical whites with splashes of warm gold (representing daughter's memories)
- Cover concept: A woman's face dissolving into particles, or a child's photograph with portions erased
- Typography: Clean, modern sans-serif — clinical and cold
