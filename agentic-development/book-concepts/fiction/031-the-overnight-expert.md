# The Overnight Expert

## Genre
Satirical Fiction / Workplace Drama / Social Commentary

## Concept

Cassandra Mills is a "knowledge influencer" — someone who builds massive online followings by appearing to be an expert on topics she learns about for approximately 72 hours before creating content. She's been a "wellness expert," a "productivity guru," a "relationship coach," and a "financial advisor" — all without formal training in any field. Her followers number in the millions. Her advice, statistically, is slightly better than chance.

When one of her followers dies following her medical "wellness advice," Cassandra faces a crisis. Not legal liability — her disclaimers are bulletproof. But existential crisis. She's spent ten years performing expertise she doesn't have, and someone died because they trusted her performance. For the first time, she questions what she does for a living.

But walking away means walking away from millions of dollars and the identity she's built. Instead, Cassandra decides on a middle path: she'll become a real expert in one thing. Just one. She picks the most boring, technical field she can find — actuarial science — and commits to actually learning it. This novel follows her journey from fake expertise to genuine competence, and what she discovers about herself, knowledge, and authenticity along the way.

## Key Themes
- The illusion of expertise in the attention economy
- The difference between knowing and performing knowing
- Responsibility in the age of influence
- What genuine mastery requires
- The appeal of shortcuts and their costs
- Redemption through actual work

## Tone
Sharp satire that becomes genuinely moving. The prose should capture influencer culture's absurdity while allowing Cassandra to become a real person.

## Unique Angle
This isn't a takedown of influencers — it's a redemption story that asks what happens when someone built on fakeness decides to become real.

## Target Audience
Readers who are exhausted by influencer culture. Fans of workplace fiction and social satire. Adults in their 30s-50s navigating the new credentialing landscape.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (13 chapters)
1. The expert — Cassandra's day-to-day performance
2. The incident — a follower dies
3. The crisis — legal safety, moral devastation
4. The decision — trying to become actually good at something
5. Day one — beginning actuarial studies
6. The difficulty — real learning is hard
7. The temptation — a new content opportunity
8. The first test — failing, trying again
9. The community — meeting real experts
10. The imposter — feeling fake among the genuine
11. The exam — proving actual competence
12. The integration — combining authenticity with platform
13. The new expert — what real confidence feels like

## Visual Identity Suggestions
- Color palette: Instagram-perfect pastels fading into authentic, earned colors
- Cover concept: A ring light with a textbook visible behind it, or a blue checkmark cracking
- Typography: Influencer-style fonts becoming more traditional and serious
