# The Un-Person

## Genre
Literary Fiction / Identity Drama / Speculative

## Concept

David Chen was a successful architect with a wife, two children, and a comfortable life in Seattle — until the day he discovered he doesn't exist. Not philosophically. Literally. His birth certificate is a forgery. His Social Security number belongs to a child who died in 1987. His college transcripts were fabricated. His entire identity is an elaborate construction, and he has no memory of building it.

Someone made David Chen from nothing, gave him a past that never happened, and inserted him into a life that shouldn't be possible. But who? And why? And if David Chen isn't real, who is the consciousness living his life?

As David investigates his own origins, he uncovers a shadowy program that creates "ghost identities" for people who need to disappear — witnesses, defectors, people fleeing impossible situations. But David wasn't running from anything he can remember. Either his memories have been altered, or he's an experiment gone wrong, or something even stranger: a person who was created fully-formed as an adult, with implanted memories of a childhood that never occurred.

## Key Themes
- What constitutes personal identity
- Memory as the foundation of self
- The bureaucratic construction of personhood
- Running from a past you can't remember
- Whether a fabricated person can have authentic relationships
- The right to know your own origins

## Tone
Existentially unsettling, philosophical but grounded. The prose should convey David's growing vertigo as every foundation of his life proves unstable.

## Unique Angle
This isn't a spy thriller — it's a meditation on what makes a person real. David's investigation is as much internal as external.

## Target Audience
Readers of literary fiction with speculative elements. Fans of Kazuo Ishiguro, Paul Auster, and Christopher Nolan films. Adults interested in philosophy of identity.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (13 chapters)
1. The document — discovering the forged birth certificate
2. The unraveling — every proof of identity fails
3. The family — what does he tell his wife?
4. The investigation — hiring someone to trace his origins
5. The program — discovering ghost identity creation exists
6. Before David — searching for who he was before
7. The blanks — periods he can't account for
8. The handler — finding someone who might know
9. The truth about memories — they may be implanted
10. The original — was there someone before David?
11. The choice — does he want to know?
12. The revelation — who David was, or wasn't
13. The person remaining — constructing identity going forward

## Visual Identity Suggestions
- Color palette: Document grays, stamp reds, official blues, existential blacks
- Cover concept: A man's reflection that shows someone different, or a birth certificate dissolving
- Typography: Official document fonts made unsettling
