# The Authenticity Plague

## Genre
Satirical Fiction / Social Horror / Speculative

## Concept

A mysterious condition is spreading through society: people are losing the ability to perform. Not stage performance — social performance. Those infected can no longer make small talk, smile when they're not happy, say "fine" when asked how they are, pretend to like people they don't, or maintain any of the thousands of daily deceptions that make social life possible. They become radically, involuntarily authentic.

The condition — dubbed "Authenticity Syndrome" or "The Realness" — spreads like a virus but can't be detected biologically. Victims lose jobs for telling clients what they actually think, destroy relationships by saying what they've always hidden, and become social pariahs because no one can tolerate unfiltered honesty.

Sophie Brennan, a public relations expert whose entire career is built on crafting acceptable lies, begins showing symptoms. As she loses the ability to spin, deflect, and perform, she must navigate a world that runs on the very falseness she can no longer produce — while also uncovering whether The Realness is a disease, an evolution, or something deliberately created to tear society apart.

## Key Themes
- The social necessity of performance
- What we call "politeness" vs. what we call "honesty"
- Whether civilization requires deception
- Authenticity as both ideal and threat
- The gap between who we are and who we present
- Social media's demand for performance vs. authenticity

## Tone
Darkly comedic, increasingly unsettling. The prose should balance satirical sharpness with genuine horror at what unfiltered truth does to relationships.

## Unique Angle
This isn't anti-authenticity — it asks genuinely whether humans can function without social performance, and whether "authenticity" is even possible or just another mask.

## Target Audience
Fans of satire and social horror. Readers of Paul Beatty, George Saunders, and Chuck Palahniuk. Adults tired of authenticity discourse who want to see it interrogated.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (13 chapters)
1. The first case — patient zero says what she thinks at a meeting
2. Sophie's world — building reputation through careful lies
3. The spread — more cases appearing
4. Symptoms — Sophie notices she can't make small talk
5. The infected — meeting others with The Realness
6. Career collapse — Sophie tells a client the truth
7. The society of the real — those who've adapted
8. Investigation — is this natural or engineered?
9. The architects — who benefits from social collapse?
10. Full infection — Sophie loses all filters
11. The choice — cure or accept?
12. The revelation — what The Realness actually is
13. The new normal — society adapting to the real

## Visual Identity Suggestions
- Color palette: Instagram-filter pastels becoming harsh unfiltered colors
- Cover concept: A smiling mask cracking to reveal raw flesh beneath, or a speech bubble with uncomfortable truth
- Typography: Clean, social-media-style fonts becoming distorted
