# The Version History

## Genre
Literary Fiction / Relationship Drama / Speculative

## Concept

What if you could see every version of a relationship — not just what happened, but what could have happened at every decision point? James and Elena have been married for twenty years, and for their anniversary, they receive an unusual gift: access to their "relationship version history," a technology that shows them the alternate timelines of their partnership. Every fight they could have handled differently. Every moment they could have been kinder. Every fork in the road where a different choice would have led somewhere else.

At first, it seems beautiful — confirming that they're in one of the better timelines, that their choices were mostly right. But as they dig deeper, they find a version of their relationship that's better. Significantly better. A timeline where James took that job in Seattle, where Elena didn't have the affair she thinks James doesn't know about, where their daughter didn't die in the accident that haunts them. A version where they're happier.

Now James and Elena must reckon with the knowledge that their actual life together is objectively inferior to what they could have had. Can they love what is when they've seen what could have been? And when James discovers that the "better" timeline has its own hidden costs, they must decide what actually makes a life together worthwhile.

## Key Themes
- The danger of comparison and optimization
- What we lose by imagining better alternatives
- Accepting the life you have vs. mourning the one you didn't
- Whether happiness can survive perfect knowledge
- The hidden costs of "better" choices
- Recommitting to an imperfect partnership

## Tone
Intimate, melancholy, ultimately affirming. The prose should feel like a long conversation between people who know each other's flaws.

## Unique Angle
This isn't about whether to leave — it's about whether to stay knowing you could have done better.

## Target Audience
Readers of relationship fiction and speculative literary work. Fans of Sally Rooney, Normal People, and thoughtful romance.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (14 chapters)
1. The anniversary — twenty years, the gift
2. The first viewing — seeing early timelines
3. The good choices — confirming they did okay
4. The better version — discovering a superior timeline
5. The comparison begins — each partner's reactions
6. The affair — James knew all along
7. The daughter — the accident that didn't happen in the other timeline
8. The resentment — could have vs. what is
9. The investigation — what's wrong with the better timeline?
10. The hidden costs — superior timeline's secrets
11. The conversation — finally talking honestly
12. The rejection — putting down the technology
13. The recommitment — choosing each other again
14. The version they choose — acceptance and continuation

## Visual Identity Suggestions
- Color palette: Warm relationship golds, alternate timeline silvers, emotional blues
- Cover concept: Two photographs overlapping slightly differently, or a road that forks and rejoins
- Typography: Elegant, romantic, slightly melancholy
