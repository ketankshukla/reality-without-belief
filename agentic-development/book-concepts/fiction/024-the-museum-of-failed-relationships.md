# The Museum of Failed Relationships

## Genre
Literary Fiction / Romance / Contemporary

## Concept

In Zagreb, Croatia, there exists a real museum called the Museum of Broken Relationships, where people donate objects from failed loves along with stories explaining their significance. Now, an American woman named Vera Santos has been hired to create a sister museum in New York City — and she's the perfect person for the job, because she's never had a relationship last longer than six months.

As Vera collects objects and stories from hundreds of strangers, she begins to see patterns in why relationships fail: the lies people tell themselves, the incompatibilities they ignore, the fears that drive them to sabotage what they want most. Each donated item comes with a story, and Vera weaves them together into an exhibition that becomes a cultural phenomenon — a cathedral to love's wreckage.

But the museum forces Vera to confront her own patterns. Why does she always leave before being left? What happened in her parents' marriage that taught her love always ends? And when she meets someone who seems different — the widower of a donor who gave the museum his late wife's wedding dress — can she break her own cycle, or is she destined to become another exhibit?

## Key Themes
- Why relationships fail and whether patterns can be broken
- Objects as vessels of emotional history
- The stories we tell about our love lives
- Fear of abandonment and self-sabotage
- Whether love can be understood or only experienced
- Curating pain into meaning

## Tone
Wistful, funny, emotionally intelligent. The prose should balance the curator's analytical distance with the raw emotion of the stories she collects.

## Unique Angle
The novel is structured around donated objects, each chapter featuring a different exhibit with its backstory — allowing multiple love stories to weave through the main narrative.

## Target Audience
Readers of literary romance and contemporary fiction. Fans of Taylor Jenkins Reid, Nick Hornby, and Rainbow Rowell. Adults interested in relationships, museums, and emotional archaeology.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (15 chapters, alternating museum exhibits with Vera's story)
1. The job — Vera is hired to create the museum
2. Exhibit: The coffee maker — story of the couple who never had mornings
3. Vera's pattern — her history of six-month relationships
4. Exhibit: The plane tickets — the trip never taken
5. Daniel arrives — the widower with the wedding dress
6. Exhibit: The wedding dress — his wife's story
7. Vera's parents — the original failed relationship
8. Exhibit: The dog collar — when the pet was all that remained
9. Connection — Vera and Daniel's slow beginning
10. Exhibit: The identical gifts — they never knew each other
11. The pattern emerges — Vera feels the urge to run
12. Exhibit: The key — fifty years of what they never said
13. Confrontation — Vera faces her own story
14. The opening — the museum launches
15. Vera's exhibit — what she finally donates

## Visual Identity Suggestions
- Color palette: Museum whites, object-specific colors, romantic faded tones
- Cover concept: A display case with a meaningful object (ring, letter, key), or two hands almost touching across a glass case
- Typography: Elegant, museum-plaque style — institutional with warmth
