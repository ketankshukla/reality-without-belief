# The Truth Eaters

## Genre
Horror / Social Commentary / Supernatural Thriller

## Concept

In the small town of Verdant Hills, people have stopped lying. Not by choice — something is making them tell the truth. Every truth. The ugly thoughts they've hidden for years, the petty resentments, the secret hatreds, the shameful desires. Marriages collapse in hours. Friendships of decades end with a single sentence. Three people have already died — one from the shock of what she heard, two from the violence that followed what they said.

Rachel Okonkwo, a journalist investigating the phenomenon, discovers the cause: creatures she comes to call Truth Eaters. They're parasitic entities that feed on deception — and they've been starving. In a world of social media performance, curated personas, and relentless spin, lies have become so sophisticated, so layered, so constant that the creatures can no longer extract nourishment. So they've evolved. Instead of feeding on lies, they now force truth out — harvesting the emotional devastation that follows.

As Verdant Hills tears itself apart, Rachel must find a way to stop the creatures while confronting an uncomfortable question: Is a world without lies actually worse? And what truths has she been hiding from herself that the creatures will eventually force out?

## Key Themes
- The social necessity of small lies and performance
- Truth as weapon vs. truth as liberation
- The horror of radical honesty
- Social media's relationship to authenticity
- What we owe each other in terms of tact and protection
- The lies we tell ourselves

## Tone
Slow-building dread escalating to social horror. The prose should feel increasingly unsafe — the reader never knows when a devastating truth will emerge. Dark humor punctuates the horror.

## Unique Angle
This inverts the typical moral framing where "truth = good." Here, truth is the monster, and the book asks genuinely uncomfortable questions about whether we need deception to function.

## Target Audience
Horror readers who enjoy social commentary. Fans of Jordan Peele, Paul Tremblay, and the "New Weird." Adults interested in philosophy of truth and social dynamics.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (13 chapters)
1. The first confession — a dinner party goes wrong
2. The spread — more uncontrolled truths emerge
3. Rachel arrives — journalist investigating the phenomenon
4. The casualties — truth's body count rises
5. The pattern — Rachel notices what triggers the events
6. First sighting — glimpsing the Truth Eaters
7. The history — creatures have existed before, in quieter times
8. The feast — creatures growing stronger as chaos spreads
9. Rachel's secret — what she's hiding from herself
10. The proposal — can humans learn to survive truth?
11. The confrontation — attempting to stop the creatures
12. The new honesty — aftermath society adjusting
13. What we keep — epilogue, uneasy equilibrium

## Visual Identity Suggestions
- Color palette: Sickeningly cheerful small-town pastels concealing rot beneath
- Cover concept: A smiling mouth with something dark crawling out, or a picturesque town with shadow creatures lurking
- Typography: Friendly, almost childish fonts made unsettling
