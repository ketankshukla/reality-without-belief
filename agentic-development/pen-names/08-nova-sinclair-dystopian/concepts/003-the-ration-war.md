# The Ration War

## Author
**Nova Sinclair** — Dystopian Fiction

---

## Genre
Dystopian / Social Collapse / Survival Fiction

---

## Concept

After the Collapse, the government didn't fall — it consolidated. The Federal Continuity Authority controls food production and distribution for the remaining population centers. Every citizen receives exactly 1,800 calories per day. No more, no less. Ration cards are life itself.

Sergeant Rosa Delgado enforces the ration system in Chicago Zone. She's seen what happens when people hoard, when they steal, when the carefully calculated distribution fails. Riots. Starvation. The collapse of everything the Authority has built. She believes in the system because she's seen the alternative.

Then she discovers the Authority is lying about production numbers.

There's enough food. There's always been enough food. The Authority has been deliberately maintaining scarcity — keeping the population dependent, grateful, controlled. The warehouses Rosa guards contain surplus that could feed everyone adequately. The hunger is manufactured.

Rosa's commanding officer notices her discovery. She's given a choice: forget what she saw, or become an enemy of the state. But Rosa has a daughter who's been sick from malnutrition, friends who've died in ration riots, a city that's suffered for a lie.

She can't forget. She has to decide what to do with knowledge that could start a revolution — or get everyone she loves killed.
