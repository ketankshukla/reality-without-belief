# Sanctuary Lies

## Author
**Nova Sinclair** — Dystopian Fiction

---

## Genre
Dystopian / Cult Survival / Escape Narrative

---

## Concept

Sanctuary promised salvation. When the world outside collapsed, Father Marcus gathered his followers behind walls of faith and concrete. For fifteen years, Sanctuary has provided: food, shelter, purpose, protection from the chaos beyond. The price is obedience. Total, unquestioning obedience.

Leah was five when her parents brought her to Sanctuary. She doesn't remember the outside world. She only knows the rules, the rituals, the absolute authority of Father Marcus. She's been a perfect believer — until she finds the journals.

Hidden beneath the chapel, journals from the community's founding reveal what Father Marcus has hidden: the collapse was never as complete as he claimed. There are other communities, functioning societies, people rebuilding. Sanctuary isn't salvation — it's isolation. Father Marcus didn't save his followers from apocalypse. He used apocalypse as cover to create a kingdom he controls absolutely.

Leah wants out. But Sanctuary's walls don't just keep the world out — they keep residents in. The community's most devout members serve as enforcers. And Father Marcus has noticed Leah's wavering faith.

She has to escape, but she can't leave alone. Her younger siblings are still believers, her mother is dying, and dozens of children born in Sanctuary have never known another option.
