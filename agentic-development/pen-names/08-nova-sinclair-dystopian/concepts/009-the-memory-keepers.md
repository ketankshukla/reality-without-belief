# The Memory Keepers

## Author
**Nova Sinclair** — Dystopian Fiction

---

## Genre
Dystopian / Memory Manipulation / Resistance Fiction

---

## Concept

After the Revision, history was rewritten. The Ministry of Truth doesn't just control information — it controls memory. Citizens undergo annual "alignment sessions" that adjust their recollections to match official history. Most people don't even know their memories have been changed. They remember what they're supposed to remember.

Mara Quinn is a Memory Keeper — one of a secret network who've found ways to resist the alignment. They keep the real history: journals written in code, stories passed mouth to ear, techniques for protecting memories from adjustment. They remember what the world was actually like before the Regime.

When Mara's younger brother returns from his first alignment session, she realizes something has gone wrong. He doesn't just have adjusted memories — he's been reprogrammed. He believes their parents were traitors. He believes Mara is dangerous. He's been turned into an informant, and he doesn't even know it.

Mara has to save her brother, protect the network, and somehow prove that the past the Regime has erased actually existed. She has to make people remember what they've been forced to forget.
