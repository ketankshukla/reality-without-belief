# The Lottery System

## Author
**Nova Sinclair** — Dystopian Fiction

---

## Genre
Dystopian / Social Horror / Survival Fiction

---

## Concept

After the Scarcity began, the government implemented the Lottery. Every year, names are drawn. Those chosen must "contribute to population optimization" — a clinical phrase for state-mandated death. It's fair, they say. Random. Equal. Everyone has the same chance.

Mira Chen has survived five Lotteries. She's watched friends, neighbors, family members drawn and taken. She's told herself what everyone tells themselves: it's necessary, it's fair, someone has to go so others can live.

Then her daughter's name is drawn.

The Lottery isn't supposed to include children under sixteen. But resources are tighter this year, and the rules have changed. Mira has thirty days to report with her daughter for "contribution." She has thirty days to find another way.

What she discovers as she fights the system is that the Lottery was never random. The algorithms that select names weight heavily against the poor, the disabled, the politically inconvenient. The wealthy and connected have never lost a child. The fairness was always a lie.

Mira can't save just her daughter — she has to break the entire system. And she has thirty days to do it.
