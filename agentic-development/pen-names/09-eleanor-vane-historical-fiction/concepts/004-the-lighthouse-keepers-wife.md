# The Lighthouse Keeper's Wife

## Author
**Eleanor Vane** — Historical Fiction

---

## Genre
Historical Fiction / Victorian Era / Isolation Drama

---

## Concept

Cornwall, 1867. When Margaret Crane marries lighthouse keeper Thomas Crane, she expects isolation. What she doesn't expect is imprisonment. The lighthouse on Serpent Rock sits three miles from shore, accessible only by boat when the seas allow. For weeks at a time, Margaret is alone with Thomas, the churning Atlantic, and secrets that the lighthouse has been keeping for decades.

Thomas is not the man she married. Away from society's gaze, his charm becomes control, his attentiveness becomes surveillance. Margaret has no way to leave, no way to send for help, no one who would believe her if she could.

Then she discovers the journal hidden in the lighthouse walls — written by the previous keeper's wife, a woman who officially "fell from the rocks" and drowned. The journal describes the same pattern: the isolation, the control, the growing fear. It also describes a plan for escape that was never completed.

Margaret realizes she's not Thomas's first wife. She may not be his second. And she has until the supply boat's next visit to find a way out — or become another woman who "fell."
