# The Mapmaker's Wife

## Author
**Eleanor Vane** — Historical Fiction

---

## Genre
Historical Fiction / Georgian Era / Exploration Drama

---

## Concept

London, 1735. When cartographer Edmund Ashworth dies in a duel over a disputed boundary line, his wife Isabella inherits his workshop, his commissions, and his secret: the maps that made Edmund famous were drawn by Isabella. For fifteen years, she's been the true mapmaker while Edmund collected the credit — and the income that allowed their household to survive.

Now Isabella faces an impossible situation. Edmund's most important commission — mapping the newly claimed territories in the American colonies — remains incomplete. The Royal Society expects delivery in six months. But a widow cannot present work to the Royal Society. A woman cannot claim expertise in cartography.

Isabella must find a man to serve as the face of her work. She hires Nathaniel Shaw, a disgraced former surveyor desperate enough to participate in the deception. Together, they'll complete Edmund's final commission: a map that will shape the future of colonial North America.

But the territories they're mapping are disputed — by French claims, Native nations, and rival British interests. The map isn't just geography. It's power. And people are willing to kill for the right to draw the lines.

---

## Key Themes
- Women's invisible labor in history
- Maps as instruments of power
- Partnership across class and gender
- Who gets credit for expertise

---

## Tone
Georgian adventure, intellectual partnership, colonial complexity.

## Unique Angle
Maps as political weapons. The mapmaker is a woman who must hide behind a male front.

6. **The Map** — Beginning the work
7. **The Territories** — Understanding what they're drawing

9. **Danger** — Enemies of the commission
10. **The Completion** — Finishing the work
11. **The Presentation** — Facing the Royal Society
12. **The Mapmaker's Wife** — Resolution
