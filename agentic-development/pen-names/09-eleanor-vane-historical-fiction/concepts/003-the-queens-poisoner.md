# The Queen's Poisoner

## Author
**Eleanor Vane** — Historical Fiction

---

## Genre
Historical Fiction / Tudor Era / Court Intrigue

---

## Concept

England, 1536. Catherine Howard is twelve years old when she's sent to serve in the household of Anne Boleyn, the doomed queen. She watches Anne's fall, her execution, her replacement. She learns the most important lesson a woman at Henry VIII's court can learn: queens are disposable. The king's favor is temporary. Survival requires being useful.

By the time Catherine becomes queen herself at sixteen, she knows exactly how dangerous her position is. She also knows something that history has forgotten: she wasn't the foolish, romantic girl the court believed her to be. She was a survivor, playing a role, making alliances, preparing for the inevitable moment when Henry tired of her.

This novel follows Catherine's story as she might have experienced it — not as a victim of circumstance but as a player who understood the game, made calculated choices, and came closer to survival than anyone realized. Her "indiscretions" weren't love affairs. They were insurance policies, alliances, escape routes.

She lost. But she played the game as well as anyone could.
