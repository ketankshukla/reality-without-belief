# The Governess of Thornfield

## Author
**Eleanor Vane** — Historical Fiction

---

## Genre
Historical Fiction / Regency Era / Gothic Romance

---

## Concept

England, 1815. After the death of her parents, Clara Ashworth takes a position as governess at Thornfield Hall, the isolated estate of Sir Edward Blackwood. The salary is generous, the child is difficult, and Sir Edward himself is a mystery — a wounded veteran who keeps to his wing of the house and communicates only through notes left on her desk.

Clara expects challenge. She doesn't expect to discover that she's the fourth governess in two years. The previous three left suddenly, without explanation, and none will answer letters. The servants speak in whispers. The child has nightmares about "the woman in the walls." And Sir Edward's notes begin to feel less like instructions and more like warnings.

As Clara investigates, she uncovers a secret that explains everything: Sir Edward's first wife didn't die, as the servants claim. She's still in the house, hidden away, driven mad by an illness he doesn't want the world to know about. He's been protecting her — and everyone else — from her violent episodes.

But Clara also discovers that the wife wasn't mad before she came to Thornfield. Something in this house made her that way. And Clara is beginning to feel it too.

---

## Key Themes
- Women's vulnerability in service positions
- Secrets and madness in great houses
- The Gothic tradition reimagined
- Agency in constrained circumstances

---

## Tone
Gothic Regency, atmospheric, romantic suspense.

---

## Unique Angle
Jane Eyre's premise reimagined: the madwoman in the attic might not have been mad when she arrived.

---

## Target Audience
Gothic romance fans, Jane Eyre admirers, historical fiction readers.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (12 chapters)

1. **Arrival** — Clara comes to Thornfield
2. **The Child** — Meeting her charge, the strangeness
3. **The Master** — Sir Edward's hidden communication
4. **The Questions** — Previous governesses, the whispers
5. **The Woman in the Walls** — Child's nightmares
6. **Investigation** — What happened to the others
7. **The Truth About the Wife** — Discovery
8. **The House** — What's wrong with Thornfield
9. **Sir Edward's Story** — His perspective, his guilt
10. **The Change** — Clara feeling effects
11. **The Escape** — Fighting the house's influence
12. **The Governess of Thornfield** — Resolution
