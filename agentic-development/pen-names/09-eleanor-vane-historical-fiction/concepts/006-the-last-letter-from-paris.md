# The Last Letter from Paris

## Author
**Eleanor Vane** — Historical Fiction

---

## Genre
Historical Fiction / World War II / Dual Timeline

---

## Concept

London, 2019. When her grandmother dies, Sophie inherits a box of letters — correspondence between her grandmother Claire and someone named "M" in occupied Paris, 1942-1944. The letters reveal a passionate love affair, resistance activities, and a promise: when the war ends, they'll meet at the Pont des Arts and start a new life together.

But Sophie's grandmother never went back to Paris. She married Sophie's grandfather, lived quietly in England, and never spoke of the war. The letters stop in August 1944, right before the liberation of Paris. What happened to M?

Sophie travels to Paris to find answers. She traces her grandmother's wartime activities through resistance archives, discovers that Claire was a courier for the SOE, and finds records of a betrayal — someone in their network gave names to the Gestapo. The last letters hint that Claire knew the traitor's identity.

As Sophie investigates, she realizes her grandmother's silence wasn't grief. It was guilt. Claire didn't just survive the war — she made choices that cost others their lives. And the man she left behind, the man she promised to meet at the bridge, was one of them.

---

## Key Themes
- War and moral compromise
- What grandchildren don't know
- Love and betrayal
- The silences that protect us from truth

---

## Tone
Dual timeline, war romance, family mystery.

---

## Unique Angle
The love story is a tragedy caused by the protagonist's own grandmother. Discovering romance means discovering guilt.

---

## Target Audience
World War II fiction readers, dual timeline fans, family mystery enthusiasts.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (12 chapters)

1. **The Inheritance** — Sophie receives the letters
2. **The Correspondence** — Reading Claire and M's love
3. **Paris, 1942** — Claire's story begins
4. **The Investigation** — Sophie goes to Paris
5. **The Resistance** — What Claire did in the war
6. **The Love Affair** — M's identity, their relationship
7. **The Betrayal** — The network is compromised
8. **The Archives** — Sophie finds the records
9. **The Names** — Who was betrayed, who betrayed them
10. **The Truth** — What Claire did
11. **The Bridge** — What happened at the Pont des Arts
12. **The Last Letter** — Resolution
