# The Forger's Daughter

## Author
**Eleanor Vane** — Historical Fiction

---

## Genre
Historical Fiction / Victorian Era / Art Mystery

---

## Concept

London, 1878. Constance Whitmore has spent her life as her father's secret. While the art world celebrates Reginald Whitmore as one of England's finest restorers of Old Masters, Constance does the actual work. Her hands repair Vermeer, her eyes assess Rembrandt, her skill is legendary — and entirely unacknowledged because a woman cannot be taken seriously in the art trade.

When her father dies suddenly, Constance inherits more than his workshop. She inherits his debts, his enemies, and a devastating secret: Reginald wasn't just restoring paintings. He was forging them. Some of the most celebrated works in London's finest collections are her father's creations — and therefore, Constance's creations.

A blackmailer knows the truth and wants payment to stay silent. The paintings in question hang in the homes of the most powerful men in England. Exposure would destroy noble families, collapse fortunes, and end with Constance in prison.

She has two choices: pay a blackmailer who will never stop demanding more, or expose the fraud herself — taking down the collectors who've built reputations on fake masterpieces, destroying her father's legacy, and proving that a woman could fool the entire art establishment.

---

## Key Themes
- Women's unrecognized talent in male-dominated fields
- Art, forgery, and the nature of value
- Legacy and inheritance of secrets
- Power and what people will do to protect reputation

## Tone
Victorian suspense, feminist undertones, art world intrigue.

## Unique Angle
