# The Captain's Widow

## Author
**Eleanor Vane** — Historical Fiction

---

## Genre
Historical Fiction / Napoleonic Era / War and Aftermath

---

## Concept

England, 1816. Captain William Ashworth died at Waterloo, leaving his wife Elizabeth with a modest pension, two children, and the gratitude of a nation that has already forgotten its dead. A year after the victory, Elizabeth struggles to maintain respectability on an income that shrinks with each passing month.

Then a stranger arrives: a French officer who served at Waterloo, carrying William's personal effects — items that were never returned through official channels. He tells Elizabeth things about her husband's final hours that the official reports never mentioned. He also tells her something else: William didn't die in battle. He was murdered by his own men.

Elizabeth doesn't want to believe it. William was beloved by his soldiers. But the French officer has evidence — a letter William was carrying, addressed to the Duke of Wellington himself, accusing superior officers of war crimes against French prisoners. William was killed to prevent that letter from being delivered.

Elizabeth faces a choice: accept her widow's pension and let her husband's murder go unpunished, or investigate and risk losing everything — her income, her reputation, her children's futures — to expose crimes that powerful men have already buried.

---

## Key Themes
- War crimes and institutional cover-ups
- Widows' limited power and options
- Truth vs. survival
- What we owe the dead

---

## Tone
Post-war drama, investigation against power, moral complexity.

---

## Unique Angle
The war hero was murdered by his own side. His widow must decide whether truth is worth her survival.

---

## Target Audience
Napoleonic era fans, war fiction readers, historical mystery enthusiasts.

6. **The Investigation** — Seeking proof
