# The Surgeon's Secret

## Author
**Eleanor Vane** — Historical Fiction

---

## Genre
Historical Fiction / Regency Era / Medical Drama

---

## Concept

Edinburgh, 1820. Dr. James Murray is the finest surgeon in Scotland — his hands steady, his diagnoses brilliant, his reputation spotless. When he dies suddenly, his assistant inherits his practice, his patients, and his secret: James Murray was born Janet Murray. For twenty years, she lived as a man to practice medicine in an age when women couldn't attend medical school, couldn't qualify as physicians, couldn't legally touch a patient.

The assistant, Robert Ashford, loved Janet. He knew her secret and protected it. Now he must protect it still — because Janet left behind a daughter, raised in secret by relatives who believe the girl's father was a distant cousin. The daughter, now sixteen, wants to study medicine like her "father."

Robert faces an impossible choice: reveal Janet's secret to inspire her daughter but destroy Janet's legacy and reputation, or keep the secret and watch a brilliant girl be denied the same opportunities her mother died to achieve. Meanwhile, a rival physician is investigating the mystery of Dr. Murray's past — and getting closer to the truth that could scandalize Edinburgh society.

---

## Key Themes
- Gender and professional limitation
- Legacy and truth
- What we owe to the dead vs. the living
- Medical history and women's exclusion

---

## Tone
Medical drama, period authenticity, secret identity.

---

## Unique Angle
The secret-keeping continues after death. The daughter deserves to know, but knowing could destroy everything.

---

## Target Audience
Historical fiction readers, medical history enthusiasts, readers interested in women's history.
