# The Spinster's Inheritance

## Author
**Eleanor Vane** — Historical Fiction

---

## Genre
Historical Fiction / Edwardian Era / Social Drama

---

## Concept

England, 1909. At thirty-five, Harriet Ashworth is firmly on the shelf — unmarried, childless, dependent on her brother's charity. Society considers her life effectively over. Then a distant relative dies and leaves Harriet a fortune: a textile mill in Manchester, a house, and enough capital to live independently for the rest of her days.

Her brother expects her to sign everything over to him. Her mother expects her to use the money to finally find a husband. Society expects her to behave as if this windfall changes nothing about her subordinate position as a woman.

Harriet has other plans. She moves to Manchester, takes over management of the mill, and discovers a world she never knew existed: working-class women whose labor built her inheritance, union organizers fighting for better conditions, suffragettes demanding votes for women. For the first time in her life, Harriet has power — and must decide how to use it.

The mill is profitable because it exploits its workers. Harriet can continue the exploitation and secure her independence, or she can reform the mill and risk losing everything to competitors who don't share her conscience.
