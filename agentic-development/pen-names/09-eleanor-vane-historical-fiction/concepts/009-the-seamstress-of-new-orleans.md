# The Seamstress of New Orleans

## Author
**Eleanor Vane** — Historical Fiction

---

## Genre
Historical Fiction / Antebellum America / Race and Identity

---

## Concept

New Orleans, 1855. Marie Laveau (no relation to the famous voodoo queen) is a free woman of color, a seamstress whose skill with needle and fabric has made her indispensable to the city's wealthiest white women. She creates gowns that launch debutantes, wedding dresses that seal fortunes, mourning clothes that properly grieve the dead. She is essential — and invisible.

When a young white woman from one of New Orleans' oldest families comes to Marie with a desperate request, everything changes. The woman, Céleste Beaumont, is secretly pregnant by a man her family would never accept. She needs Marie to help her hide the pregnancy, deliver the child in secret, and find someone to take the baby — someone of color, because the child's father was not white.

Marie knows this story. She's lived a version of it. Her own mother was a white woman who couldn't acknowledge her mixed-race child. Marie was raised by the woman who should have been her aunt, never knowing her mother's identity until it was too late.

Helping Céleste means confronting Marie's own history. It also means entering the dangerous space between the races, where a free woman of color can be stripped of her freedom for helping the wrong person — or knowing too much.

- The intimate knowledge of servants

2. **The Request** — Céleste's desperate ask

4. **The Agreement** — Deciding to help
5. **The Hiding** — Concealing the pregnancy
6. **The Investigation** — Céleste's family suspects
7. **The Danger** — What happens to those who know too much
8. **The Birth** — The child arrives
9. **The Placement** — Finding a home for the baby
10. **The Reckoning** — Truths that emerge
11. **The Seamstress of New Orleans** — Resolution
