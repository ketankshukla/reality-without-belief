# The Hollowing

## Author
**Silas Crow** — Supernatural Horror

---

## Genre
Supernatural Horror / Haunted House / Cosmic Horror

---

## Concept

The Morrow house has been in Daniel's family for four generations. When he inherits it after his grandmother's death, he plans to sell it — he has no use for a decrepit Victorian in a town he hasn't visited since childhood. But the house has other plans.

Something lives in the walls.

Not rats. Not squirrels. Something that's been there longer than the house itself. Something that was old when Daniel's great-great-grandfather built on this land, and that took up residence in the spaces between the walls because it liked what the family brought: warmth, life, voices. Things it had been without for a very long time.

Daniel doesn't notice at first. Just sounds at night — scratching, whispering, the sense of presence. Then the hollowing begins. The walls thin. Rooms that were square become not-quite. Distances inside the house stop matching distances outside. And the thing in the walls grows closer, pressing against the plaster, reaching through in ways that leave marks but no holes.

His grandmother lived here for sixty years. She knew how to keep it satisfied. She knew the rules, the offerings, the words that held it back. Daniel knows nothing. And the thing is hungry.

## Key Themes
- Houses that are more than houses
- Inheritance of obligation alongside property
- The old things that predate us
- Containment failing

---

## Tone
Slow dread, architectural wrongness, cosmic indifference.

---

## Unique Angle
The house isn't haunted — it's infested with something older than haunting. The previous owner kept it in check.

---

## Target Audience
Haunted house fans, readers of Shirley Jackson, cosmic horror enthusiasts.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (10 chapters)

1. **Inheritance** — Daniel returns to claim the house
2. **First Night** — Sounds in the walls
3. **The Thinning** — Noticing wrongness in geometry
4. **Research** — Grandmother's journals, hints of rules
5. **Presence** — Direct encounters with what's inside
6. **The History** — What was here before the house
7. **The Rules** — Understanding what grandmother did
8. **Breaking Down** — Daniel's attempts failing
9. **The Reaching** — It presses through
10. **The Hollowing** — Final confrontation
