# The Whispering Season

## Author
**Silas Crow** — Supernatural Horror

---

## Genre
Supernatural Horror / Folk Horror / Seasonal Horror

---

## Concept

Every autumn, the town of Harrow's End gets quieter. Not peaceful — quieter. Conversations become whispers. Dogs stop barking. Children play in silence. The residents barely notice anymore; it's just how October is.

Every spring, a family is gone. Their house stands empty. Their belongings remain. No one remembers them leaving. After a few weeks, no one remembers them at all.

Mira Blackwood returns to Harrow's End after fifteen years away, summoned by her father's death. She left as a teenager, fled really, from a town that felt wrong in ways she couldn't explain. Now she's back to settle the estate, planning to leave before October begins.

But October comes early this year. The whispering starts in late September. And this year, for the first time anyone can remember, Mira can hear what the whispers are saying.

They're names. Every name in the town, being spoken over and over, as if something is taking attendance. Making a list. Choosing.

Mira has until spring to understand what's been taking families from Harrow's End. She has until the whispers say her name to find a way to stop it. And she has to do it in a town where everyone has forgotten that anyone was ever taken at all.

---

## Key Themes
- Collective forgetting as horror
- Seasonal cycles of predation
- Returning to places we fled
- What towns sacrifice to continue

---

## Tone
Folk horror, creeping seasonal dread, whispers at the edge of hearing.

---

## Unique Angle
The horror is annual, accepted, forgotten. No one fights it because no one remembers there's anything to fight.

---

## Target Audience
Folk horror fans, seasonal horror enthusiasts, readers who love atmosphere.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (10 chapters)

1. **Return** — Mira comes back to Harrow's End
2. **The Quiet** — October beginning early
3. **The Whispers** — Hearing what others can't
4. **The Pattern** — Understanding the annual disappearances
5. **The Forgotten** — Proof of families no one remembers
6. **The History** — How long this has been happening
7. **The List** — Understanding how the choosing works
8. **Her Name** — Hearing herself whispered
9. **Breaking the Cycle** — Attempting to end it
10. **The Whispering Season** — Resolution
