# The Mourning House

## Author
**Silas Crow** — Supernatural Horror

---

## Genre
Supernatural Horror / Grief Horror / Haunted House

---

## Concept

Three months after her daughter died, Laura Mercer buys the house where it happened. Everyone thinks she's lost her mind — what mother returns to the site of her child's death? But Laura has a reason. In the weeks since the accident, she's seen Lily. Not memories. Not grief hallucinations. Her daughter, standing in the yard of the old house, looking up at the window of the room where she died.

Laura moves in to be closer to whatever remains of her child. And the house welcomes her.

At first, it's everything she hoped. Signs of Lily everywhere — toys moved, drawings appearing, the sound of small footsteps. Laura knows she should be afraid, but she isn't. This is her daughter. This is connection. This is what she needs.

But the house is showing her only what she wants to see. The real Lily — whatever's left of her — is trapped, and what's pretending to be her daughter is something far older. Something that feeds on grief, that lures mourning parents to houses where children have died, that uses the dead as bait and the living as sustenance.

By the time Laura realizes the truth, she's already given the house too much of herself. And it's not going to let her leave with anything intact.

---

## Key Themes
- Grief as vulnerability
- What we'll believe because we need to
- Houses that prey on mourning
- The trap of wanting to stay connected

---

## Tone
Grief-soaked horror, maternal desperation, slow revelation of the trap.

---

## Unique Angle
The protagonist knows she shouldn't be here but can't leave. The horror is what she's willing to accept to stay close to her daughter.

---

## Target Audience
Grief horror fans, haunted house enthusiasts, readers who appreciate emotional horror.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (10 chapters)

1. **The Purchase** — Laura buys the house
2. **Moving In** — First signs of Lily
3. **Connection** — Evidence of her daughter
4. **The Routine** — Living with the haunting
5. **Doubts** — Small things that don't fit
6. **The History** — Other families who came here
7. **The Truth** — Understanding what the house is
8. **Trapped** — Trying to leave
9. **The Daughter That Isn't** — Confronting the mimicry
10. **The Mourning House** — Resolution
