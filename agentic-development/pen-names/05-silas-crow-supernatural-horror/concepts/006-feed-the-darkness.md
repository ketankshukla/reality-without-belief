# Feed the Darkness

## Author
**Silas Crow** — Supernatural Horror

---

## Genre
Supernatural Horror / Possession Horror / Blackout Horror

---

## Concept

The blackouts started small. A few seconds of lost time, like spacing out in a conversation. Marcus Reeves barely noticed them — work stress, not enough sleep, the usual explanations. Then they lasted minutes. Then hours. Then he started waking up in places he didn't remember going, wearing clothes he didn't recognize, with blood under his fingernails.

Something is using Marcus during the gaps. Something that needs a body to do things it can't do without one. And each time it borrows him, the blackouts get longer.

Marcus tries everything: sleep studies, brain scans, therapy. The doctors find nothing wrong. But he starts documenting the gaps — setting up cameras, checking his phone's location history, asking neighbors what they saw. The evidence paints a picture of a man who goes out at night, walks to specific locations, performs actions that look ritualistic, and returns before dawn.

The thing in the darkness is building something. Using Marcus's hands. Working toward a goal that requires weeks of preparation. And as the final date approaches, Marcus realizes the blackouts aren't getting longer randomly — they're scheduled. Whatever is using him is almost done. And the last blackout, the one coming in three days, won't be temporary.

## Key Themes
- Losing control of your own body
- The horror of not knowing what you've done
- Being the tool of something inhuman
- Racing against yourself

---

## Tone
Paranoid, desperate, loss of bodily autonomy.

---

## Unique Angle
Possession through time, not space. The thing doesn't share the body — it takes turns.

---

## Target Audience
Psychological horror fans, possession horror enthusiasts, readers who fear loss of control.

---
