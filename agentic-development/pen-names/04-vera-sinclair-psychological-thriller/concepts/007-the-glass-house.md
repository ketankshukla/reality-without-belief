# The Glass House

## Author
**Vera Sinclair** — Psychological Thriller

---

## Genre
Psychological Thriller / Marriage Suspense / Isolation Thriller

---

## Concept

The house has no curtains. Floor-to-ceiling windows on every wall. Total transparency — that was the architect's vision. It's also why no one has lived in it for more than two years.

When Rachel's husband Adam suggests they move to the Glass House, she thinks it's his way of rebuilding their marriage after his affair. A fresh start with no secrets. No walls to hide behind. Everything visible.

But living in glass changes you. Rachel can see every approach, every car, every person. She can also be seen. Always. The feeling of being observed that never stops.

Adam says she's paranoid. But Rachel finds photographs taken from outside — of her, in moments she thought were private. Someone is watching. And when she discovers that every previous resident reported the same paranoia before leaving, she realizes the house isn't just transparent. It's a trap.

Someone designed it that way. And Adam knew exactly what this house was when he brought her here.

---

## Key Themes
- Transparency as prison
- Gaslighting in architecture
- Marriage as control
- The watcher and the watched

---

## Tone
Claustrophobic despite openness. Paranoia justified.

---

## Unique Angle
The house itself is the weapon. Designed to create the exact psychological state the protagonist experiences.

---

## Target Audience
Readers who love architectural horror, marriage thrillers, isolation suspense.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (10 chapters)

1. **The Move** — Arriving at the Glass House
2. **Transparency** — Learning to live exposed
3. **The Feeling** — First sense of being watched
4. **Adam's Reassurance** — He says she's paranoid
5. **The Photographs** — Proof someone is watching
6. **Previous Residents** — Learning she's not the first
7. **The Design** — Understanding the house's purpose
8. **Adam's Truth** — What he knew
9. **Trapped** — No way to hide, no way to leave
10. **The Glass House** — Confrontation, resolution
