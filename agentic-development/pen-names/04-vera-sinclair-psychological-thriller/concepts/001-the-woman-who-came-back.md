# The Woman Who Came Back

## Author
**Vera Sinclair** — Psychological Thriller

---

## Genre
Psychological Thriller / Domestic Suspense / Amnesia Thriller

---

## Concept

Claire Ashworth survived the accident with no memory of the year before it. The doctors call it retrograde amnesia — her brain protecting itself from trauma. Her husband calls it a blessing. "You don't want to remember that year," he tells her. "Trust me."

But Claire has questions. Why did she have a second phone in her purse, one her husband says he's never seen? Why do her colleagues at work treat her differently than before — some warmer, others suddenly cold? And why, three weeks after she comes home from the hospital, does someone try to run her off the same stretch of road where she nearly died?

As Claire investigates the year she's lost, she discovers a woman she doesn't recognize: someone who was keeping secrets, making plans, preparing for something. Someone who might have had very good reasons to be afraid.

The accident wasn't an accident. Someone wanted to kill the woman Claire used to be. And that woman knew it was coming — which is why she left clues for herself, hidden where only she would find them.

The question is whether Claire can solve her own murder before the killer realizes she's starting to remember.

---

## Key Themes
- Identity and the self we can't remember
- Marriage secrets and what spouses don't know
- Investigating your own past
- Who were you before you became who you are now?

---

## Tone
Paranoid, layered, with reveals that reframe everything.

---

## Unique Angle
The protagonist investigating herself. The murder victim and the detective are the same person.

---

## Target Audience
Readers of *Before I Go to Sleep*, Gillian Flynn, amnesia thrillers with domestic settings.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (12 chapters)

1. **The Hospital** — Waking up with no memory
2. **Coming Home** — Everything familiar, everything wrong
3. **The Phone** — First clue, first lies
4. **The Year She Lost** — Piecing together the timeline
5. **The Attempt** — Someone tries again
6. **The Other Claire** — Discovering who she was becoming
7. **The Clues She Left** — Realizing past-Claire anticipated this
8. **The Suspects** — Who wanted her dead?
9. **The Marriage** — What was really happening at home
10. **Remembering** — Fragments returning
11. **The Truth** — What she discovered that year
12. **The Woman Who Came Back** — Confrontation, resolution
