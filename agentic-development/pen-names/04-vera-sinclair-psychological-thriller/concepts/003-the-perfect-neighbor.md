# The Perfect Neighbor

## Author
**Vera Sinclair** — Psychological Thriller

---

## Genre
Psychological Thriller / Domestic Suspense / Neighbor Thriller

---

## Concept

When Sophie and Mark Chen move into their new house, the first person to welcome them is Dana Aldridge from next door. She's warm, helpful, always available — she brings casseroles, offers to collect packages, knows everything about the neighborhood.

At first, Sophie is grateful. New city, no friends, Mark working long hours. Dana becomes her lifeline. Coffee mornings, wine evenings, someone who actually listens.

Then Sophie notices things. Dana knows details Sophie never shared. She's seen Dana watching from her window at odd hours. And when Sophie mentions a private conversation she had with Mark — one that happened inside their house — Dana responds to it. As if she heard every word.

Sophie starts to pull away. Dana doesn't let her. The helpful neighbor becomes omnipresent: always there when Sophie opens her door, always commenting on Sophie's plans before she's announced them, always so friendly it's impossible to explain why it feels threatening.

When Sophie finally confronts her, Dana's response is calm, reasonable, and utterly terrifying: "I just want to take care of you. The last family who lived there needed someone to take care of them too."

Sophie investigates the previous owners. They left suddenly. No forwarding address. Their realtor won't answer questions. And Dana is still watching.

---

## Key Themes
- The horror of excessive friendliness
- Surveillance disguised as care
- Trusting instincts vs. appearing ungrateful
- What happened to the people before

---

## Tone
Slow-building paranoia. Dana is so helpful that Sophie doubts her own discomfort.

---

## Unique Angle
The threat is kindness. Dana never does anything obviously wrong — which makes her impossible to escape.

---

## Target Audience
Readers who love neighbor thrillers, gaslighting narratives, domestic horror.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (11 chapters)

1. **Moving In** — The new house, first meeting Dana
2. **The Welcome** — Dana's overwhelming helpfulness
3. **Gratitude** — Sophie appreciating the friendship
4. **Small Things** — First unsettling observations
5. **Always There** — Dana's omnipresence
6. **The Listening** — Realizing Dana knows too much
7. **Pulling Away** — Sophie's attempt to create distance
8. **Dana Responds** — The resistance to being distanced
9. **The Previous Family** — Investigating who lived here before
10. **The Confrontation** — Facing Dana
11. **The Perfect Neighbor** — Resolution
