# Trust No One

## Author
**Vera Sinclair** — Psychological Thriller

---

## Genre
Psychological Thriller / Amnesia Thriller / Gaslighting Suspense

---

## Concept

Ellie wakes up in a hospital with no memory. A man claiming to be her husband sits beside her bed, explaining that she tried to kill herself. The doctors confirm it: self-inflicted wounds, a history of depression, a note left behind.

But in Ellie's hand, hidden from everyone, is another note. One she doesn't remember writing. Three words in her own handwriting: "Don't trust him."

She doesn't know who "him" refers to. The husband? The doctor? Someone else entirely? She only knows that past-Ellie, the woman who existed before the memory loss, wanted present-Ellie to be afraid.

As she recovers, Ellie investigates her own life. Her husband is attentive, loving, patient with her confusion. Her friends describe a happy marriage. Everyone says she was struggling with depression, that the suicide attempt makes tragic sense.

But small inconsistencies pile up. Her husband knows things he shouldn't. Her "friends" react strangely to questions. And the handwriting analysis she secretly commissions confirms the worst: the suicide note wasn't written by her. Someone else wrote it.

Someone tried to kill her and make it look like suicide. That person is still in her life, pretending to love her. And Ellie has no idea which face hides the killer.

---

## Key Themes
- Trusting yourself when memory fails
- Gaslighting at its most extreme
- The vulnerability of not knowing your own life
- Past self protecting present self

---

## Tone
Paranoid, gaslit, with reveals that validate the paranoia.

---

## Unique Angle
Two notes — one official, one hidden. Past-Ellie knew something and left a warning.

---

## Target Audience
Readers who love amnesia thrillers, gaslighting narratives, "trust no one" premises.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (11 chapters)

1. **Waking Up** — Hospital, no memory, the husband
2. **The Note** — Finding "Don't trust him"
3. **Recovery** — Learning about her life
4. **The Perfect Husband** — Everything he does seems right
5. **Inconsistencies** — Small things don't add up
6. **Investigation** — Secretly digging into her past
7. **The Handwriting** — The suicide note wasn't hers
8. **Suspects** — Who tried to kill her?
9. **Remembering** — Fragments returning
10. **The Truth** — Understanding what happened
11. **Trust No One** — Confrontation, resolution
