# Before She Disappears

## Author
**Vera Sinclair** — Psychological Thriller

---

## Genre
Psychological Thriller / Gothic Suspense / House Horror

---

## Concept

Every woman who has lived in Ashworth House has eventually left without saying goodbye. The realtor mentioned it casually — "high turnover" — but didn't explain that four women in forty years have simply vanished from the property. No bodies. No evidence of crime. Just women who were there one day and gone the next.

Margot Webb knows this when she moves in. She's researched the house extensively. She's a true crime writer, and Ashworth House is her next book — if she can figure out what happened to the women who lived there before her.

At first, the house seems normal. Old, yes. Creaky. Full of locked rooms and sealed-over doors that previous owners never bothered to open. But as Margot settles in, she starts finding things: a journal hidden in the walls. Photographs with faces scratched out. A calendar marking dates that correspond to when each woman vanished.

The pattern becomes clear: each woman lasted approximately eighteen months. Margot has been here for sixteen.

She has two months to solve a forty-year mystery, or become its latest chapter. The problem is, the closer she gets to the truth, the more the house seems determined to keep its secrets — and keep her.

---

## Key Themes
- Investigating becomes participating
- Houses that hold secrets (and people)
- The patterns that nobody notices
- Deadline-driven suspense

---

## Tone
Gothic thriller with countdown tension. The house is a character.

---

## Unique Angle
She moved in knowing the history. She's investigating, not stumbling into danger.

---

## Target Audience
Readers who love haunted house thrillers, true crime elements, countdown narratives.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (12 chapters)

1. **The Move** — Margot arriving, knowing the history
2. **The Research** — What she knows about the vanished women
3. **Settling In** — First weeks, normal enough
4. **The Discoveries** — Finding hidden journals, photographs
5. **The Pattern** — Understanding the timeline
6. **Sixteen Months** — Realizing where she is in the cycle
7. **Deeper Investigation** — Interviewing locals, researching history
8. **The House Responds** — Strange occurrences, sealed rooms
9. **The Truth Emerges** — What happened to the women
10. **Seventeen Months** — Time running out
11. **The Trap** — Understanding how the house works
12. **Before She Disappears** — Confrontation, resolution
