# What He Didn't Know

## Author
**Vera Sinclair** — Psychological Thriller

---

## Genre
Psychological Thriller / Double Life / Marriage Suspense

---

## Concept

For three years, Nina Chen has been living a second life. Different name, different apartment, different everything — a complete identity her husband knows nothing about. She tells herself it's not an affair, not really. It's just space. A place where she's not someone's wife, someone's employee, someone's daughter. A place where she's only herself.

Then her husband finds out.

But he doesn't confront her. He doesn't rage or cry or demand explanations. He doesn't say anything at all. He just... knows. And he starts behaving differently. Small changes. A comment that references something from her other life. A gift that could only make sense if he knew. A smile that feels like a threat.

Nina realizes she's being watched. Her husband is documenting everything — building something, preparing for something. And the longer he stays silent, the more terrifying his eventual response becomes.

She wanted a life he couldn't touch. Now she's trapped between two identities, both of which he controls.

---

## Key Themes
- Secret lives and their costs
- Silence as weapon
- Who controls the narrative of a marriage
- The terror of waiting for consequences

---

## Tone
Slow-building dread. His silence is the horror.

---

## Unique Angle
He knows, she knows he knows, but he never acknowledges it. The confrontation never comes — which is worse.

---

## Target Audience
Readers who love double-life thrillers, marriages as battlegrounds, psychological warfare.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (10 chapters)

1. **The Other Life** — Nina's second identity, why she built it
2. **Discovery** — Realizing he knows
3. **Silence** — His non-reaction
4. **Small Signs** — Changes in his behavior
5. **Watching Back** — Nina investigating what he's doing
6. **The Documentation** — Finding his records
7. **Trapped** — Both lives becoming prisons
8. **What He's Planning** — Understanding his endgame
9. **The Confrontation That Never Comes** — Breaking point
10. **What He Didn't Know** — Final revelation
