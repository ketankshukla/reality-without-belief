# Ghosts of the Undermarket

## Author
**Jett Holloway** — Urban Fantasy

---

## Genre
Urban Fantasy / Supernatural Noir / Heist Fantasy

---

## Concept

Beneath every major city, there's another city: the Undermarket. A supernatural black market where anything can be bought or sold — cursed objects, demon contracts, memories, years of life, the services of things that shouldn't exist. The Undermarket has been neutral territory for centuries, governed by its own laws and its own enforcers.

Jack Morrow used to be one of those enforcers. Now he's a thief who specializes in stealing from the Undermarket itself — a crime so audacious that most supernatural entities don't believe it's possible.

His latest job: steal the Ledger of Souls, a book that records every debt owed in the Undermarket. The client is a demon who claims he just wants to wipe his own entry. The reality is that the Ledger could collapse the entire supernatural economy — and someone is willing to pay enough to make the end of the world profitable.

Jack doesn't care about supernatural politics. He cares about the score. But when the heist goes wrong and he ends up reading his own entry in the Ledger, he discovers he owes a debt he doesn't remember incurring — one that's coming due very soon.

## Key Themes
- Supernatural economies and debt
- The thief who steals from thieves
- Debts forgotten but not forgiven
- Heist gone wrong

---

## Tone
Supernatural noir, heist thriller, dark humor.

---

## Unique Angle
Heist in a supernatural black market. The target is the record of all supernatural debts.

---

## Target Audience
Urban fantasy fans who like heist narratives and underworld settings.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (11 chapters)

1. **The Undermarket** — Introduction to the world
2. **The Job** — The Ledger heist proposed
3. **The Team** — Assembling the crew
4. **The Plan** — How to steal the impossible
5. **The Execution** — Heist in progress
6. **The Complication** — Things go wrong
7. **The Ledger** — Jack reads his own entry
8. **The Debt** — What he owes, who holds it
9. **The Client** — True motives revealed
10. **The Chase** — Everyone wants the Ledger
11. **Ghosts of the Undermarket** — Resolution
