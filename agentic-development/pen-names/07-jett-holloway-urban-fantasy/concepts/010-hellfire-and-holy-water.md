# Hellfire and Holy Water

## Author
**Jett Holloway** — Urban Fantasy

---

## Genre
Urban Fantasy / Angel vs Demon / Supernatural Action

---

## Concept

Theo Valentine was a priest until he discovered the Church was lying about everything. Not the faith parts — the operational parts. The secret war between Heaven and Hell isn't about souls. It's about territory, resources, and power. Both sides are playing humanity like chess pieces, and neither actually cares who wins.

Armed with stolen Church intelligence and a list of very angry contacts on both sides, Theo has become something new: a neutral party who plays angels and demons against each other for profit and, occasionally, to protect the humans caught in between.

His latest job puts him between a fallen angel who wants to defect back to Heaven and a demon prince who wants to stop her. Both are offering him everything he's ever wanted. Both are lying. And somewhere in the middle is a human girl who's the real prize — a child who can see the true forms of both celestial beings, a power that could tip the balance of the war.

Theo has to get the girl to safety while navigating two supernatural armies. He has no allies, no backup, and no good options. Just a flask of holy water, a lighter blessed in hellfire, and the knowledge that everyone is going to betray him.

## Key Themes
- The corruption of celestial powers
- Neutrality in supernatural war
- Protecting innocents in cosmic conflict
- Trust no one, especially the holy

## Tone
Action-heavy, cynical, dark humor about religion.

---

## Unique Angle
Both angels and demons are corrupt. The protagonist plays both sides.

## Target Audience
Urban fantasy fans who like morally complex supernatural beings and action.

4. **The Child** — The real prize
