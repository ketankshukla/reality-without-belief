# Dead Man's Debt

## Author
**Jett Holloway** — Urban Fantasy

---

## Genre
Urban Fantasy / Supernatural Action / Dark Fantasy

---

## Concept

Marcus Cole died three years ago. He remembers it clearly — the demon's claws, the pain, the darkness. What he doesn't remember is making a deal with something worse to come back. Now he's alive again, working as a supernatural debt collector in Chicago, recovering what's owed to the entities that exist between worlds.

The job is simple: find people who've made deals with demons, spirits, or worse, and collect what they promised. Sometimes it's money. Sometimes it's objects. Sometimes it's years of life, memories, or firstborn children. Marcus doesn't judge — he just collects.

Then he's assigned to collect from a ten-year-old girl whose desperate mother made a deal to save her life. The girl owes a demon seven years of servitude. Marcus has three days to deliver her.

For the first time since he came back, Marcus refuses a collection. But in his business, you don't say no. The entity that brought him back has ways of enforcing compliance, and the demon owed the debt has ways of making defaulters suffer. Marcus is about to learn exactly what his resurrection cost — and whether he can pay a different price to save a child he's never met.

---

## Key Themes
- The cost of second chances
- What we owe for what we receive
- Finding lines you won't cross
- The morality of supernatural systems

---

## Tone
Noir urban fantasy, morally complex, darkly humorous.

---

## Unique Angle
Supernatural debt collector forced to refuse a collection. The system he serves becomes his enemy.

---

## Target Audience
Dresden Files fans, urban fantasy readers who like moral complexity.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (11 chapters)

1. **The Collection** — Marcus's work, a typical job
2. **The Assignment** — The girl, her history
3. **The Refusal** — Marcus says no
4. **Consequences** — His boss responds
5. **The Mother** — Understanding the original deal
6. **Running** — Hiding the girl
7. **The Hunt** — Demon and employer both pursuing
8. **The Cost** — What Marcus's resurrection requires
9. **The Alternative** — Finding another way
10. **The Confrontation** — Facing what he owes
11. **Dead Man's Debt** — Resolution
