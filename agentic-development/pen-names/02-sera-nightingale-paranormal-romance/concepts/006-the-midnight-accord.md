# The Midnight Accord

## Author
**Sera Nightingale** — Paranormal Romance

---

## Genre
Paranormal Romance / Fae Bargain Romance / Dark Romance

---

## Concept

At the stroke of midnight on her twenty-fifth birthday, Iris Fairfax made a bargain she doesn't remember. She knows this because she woke up the next morning with a mark on her wrist that wasn't there before — and a stranger in her apartment who claims she owes him a debt.

His name is Cael. He's old in ways that have nothing to do with appearance. And according to him, Iris traded something precious at the Midnight Market — a supernatural bazaar that exists only between one breath and the next — in exchange for her heart's deepest desire.

The problem is, Iris doesn't know what she asked for. The Market erases memory of the transaction. She only knows what she gave up: one year of her life, to be collected in service at a time of Cael's choosing.

Now Cael is collecting. He needs Iris for a task in the Fae Courts — something only a human can do, though he won't say what. She has no choice but to follow him into a world where bargains are law, desire is currency, and the fae lord who owns her time is the only thing standing between her and creatures far worse.

But as Iris works off her debt, she starts remembering. Flashes of the Market. Glimpses of what she asked for. And the terrible, wonderful truth: she didn't ask for wealth or power or love. She asked for Cael. She asked to meet him.

---

## Key Themes
- Forgotten bargains and rediscovered desire
- Debt and freedom in supernatural contracts
- Trust when memory fails
- Wanting someone you're bound to

---

## Tone
Mystery-romance blend. The forgotten bargain drives both plot and tension.

---

## Unique Angle
She made the bargain deliberately but doesn't remember — the mystery is discovering her own choices.

---

## Target Audience
Readers who love bargain romance, mystery elements, and slow-burn with debt dynamics.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (12 chapters)

1. **The Mark** — Waking with the brand, the stranger
2. **The Terms** — Learning what she owes
3. **Into the Courts** — Following Cael into Fae realms
4. **The Task** — What he needs her for
5. **First Memories** — Flashes of the Market returning
6. **Working Together** — Forced partnership, friction
7. **The Courts' Dangers** — Others notice her, want her
8. **Growing Trust** — Relying on each other
9. **The Memory Returns** — Discovering what she actually asked for
10. **The Truth** — Cael's reaction, what he feels
11. **The Final Task** — Completing the debt
12. **The Midnight Accord** — Resolution; HEA
