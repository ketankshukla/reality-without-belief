# Queen of the Hollow Court

## Author
**Sera Nightingale** — Paranormal Romance

---

## Genre
Paranormal Romance / Fae Romance / Dark Fantasy Romance

---

## Concept

When Ivy Marchetti's grandmother dies, she leaves Ivy a single inheritance: a debt. According to a contract written in blood and starlight, Ivy's grandmother borrowed three nights of Fae magic fifty years ago — and the price was that her granddaughter would spend three nights in the Hollow Court when she came of age.

Ivy doesn't believe in Fae. She's a practical woman with a practical job and a practical apartment in Chicago. Then the Wild Hunt arrives at her door, and she learns that belief is irrelevant. The contract is binding. She's going.

The Hollow Court is everything the legends warned about: beautiful and treacherous, governed by rules that make no sense and punishments that make too much. Its king, Ronan of the Endless Dark, is ancient, terrifying, and irritatingly amused by the mortal woman who argues with everything he says.

Three nights. Ivy just has to survive three nights without eating the food, accepting any gifts, or telling any lies. Simple enough, except the Court is in the middle of a succession crisis, and Ronan's enemies have noticed that the mortal woman he's bound to receive might be useful leverage. Suddenly, surviving three nights means navigating Fae politics, avoiding assassination attempts, and resisting an attraction to a king who definitely doesn't have her best interests at heart.

But Ronan is hiding something about why he wanted Ivy specifically. Her grandmother's debt wasn't random. And the three nights might be the beginning of something much longer than Ivy ever agreed to.

## Key Themes
- Contracts and consent in supernatural bargains
- Fish-out-of-water in dangerous courts
- Attraction to someone you shouldn't trust
- Legacy debts and family secrets

---

## Tone
Witty and tense. Ivy's practicality against Fae inscrutability creates friction and chemistry.

---

## Unique Angle
Modern woman, ancient court. The clash of contemporary sensibility with timeless Fae politics.

---

## Target Audience
Readers who love snarky heroines, imperious fae lords, and forced proximity romance.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (12 chapters)

1. **The Inheritance** — Grandmother's death, the debt revealed
2. **The Hunt Arrives** — No escape from the contract
3. **First Night: Arrival** — Entry into the Hollow Court
4. **The Rules** — Learning what she can and cannot do
5. **The King** — First real encounter with Ronan
6. **Court Politics** — The succession crisis, the dangers
7. **Second Night: Danger** — Assassination attempt, Ronan's protection
8. **Closer Than Safe** — Forced proximity, growing tension
9. **The Secret** — Why Ronan wanted her specifically
10. **Third Night: Choice** — Final night, final test
11. **Breaking the Contract** — What she's willing to give up
12. **Queen of the Hollow Court** — Resolution; HEA or HFN
