# A Court of Stolen Stars

## Author
**Sera Nightingale** — Paranormal Romance

---

## Genre
Paranormal Romance / Fae Romance / Fantasy Romance

---

## Concept

Elara Thornwood is a cartographer — one of the last who still knows how to map the spaces between worlds. When the boundaries between the mortal realm and the Fae Courts begin to shift, she's hired by a desperate human lord to chart the changes before his lands are swallowed entirely.

What she doesn't expect is Caelum, High Lord of the Shadowmere Court, appearing in her workshop at midnight with a different kind of offer. The Fae Courts are dying. The magic that sustains them is draining away through wounds in reality that only a mortal mapmaker can see. He needs her to find the source of the bleeding — and he's willing to pay any price she names.

Elara names her price: safe passage for her younger sister, who was stolen by the Fae twenty years ago and has been trapped in the Courts ever since. Caelum agrees too quickly, and Elara knows enough about Fae bargains to understand that means the task is more dangerous than he's admitting.

As she travels deeper into the Courts, mapping territories no mortal has seen in centuries, she discovers that the wounds aren't accidental. Someone is deliberately killing the Fae realms — and Caelum knows who. He's brought her here not just to map the damage, but to help him destroy the one responsible: his own brother, the king who stole her sister in the first place.

The problem is, the more time Elara spends with Caelum, the harder it becomes to remember that he's using her. And the more she learns about Fae politics, the less certain she is about who the real villain is.

---

## Key Themes
- Trust and deception in Fae bargains
- Power imbalances in supernatural romance
- Family loyalty vs. dangerous attraction
- Mapping the unmappable (literally and emotionally)

---

## Tone
Lush and dangerous. The Fae Courts are beautiful and wrong. The romance is slow-burn with genuine tension.

---

## Unique Angle
The protagonist's profession (cartographer) is essential to the plot. She's not a fighter but a mapper — her power is in seeing what others miss.

---

## Target Audience
Readers of Sarah J. Maas, Holly Black, Jennifer L. Armentrout who want fae romance with stakes.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (14 chapters)

1. **The Shifting Borders** — Elara's work, the anomalies she's noticing
2. **The Midnight Visitor** — Caelum's offer
3. **The Bargain** — Terms set, sister as price
4. **Into Shadowmere** — First entry into the Fae Courts
5. **The First Wound** — Mapping the damage, feeling the wrongness
6. **Court Politics** — Meeting Caelum's court, sensing the tensions
7. **The Sister's Prison** — Finding where her sister is held
8. **Growing Closer** — Forced proximity, reluctant attraction
9. **The Brother King** — Learning about Caelum's sibling, the true enemy
10. **The Source** — Discovering what's causing the wounds
11. **Betrayal Revealed** — Caelum's full agenda exposed
12. **The Choice** — Sister's freedom vs. the Courts' survival
13. **The Confrontation** — Facing the king
14. **A Court of Stolen Stars** — Resolution; HEA earned
