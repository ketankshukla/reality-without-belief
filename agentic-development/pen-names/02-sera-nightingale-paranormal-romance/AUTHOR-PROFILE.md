## Short Bio (150 words)

Sera Nightingale writes paranormal romance about mortal women who stumble into worlds they were never meant to see — and fall for the dangerous, immortal beings who rule them. Her fae courts are not playgrounds. They are political, ancient, and hungry. Her love interests are powerful, morally complex, and genuinely dangerous. And her slow burns are designed to make readers physically uncomfortable with unresolved tension before the payoff.

Sera believes the best romances are the ones where falling in love feels like a terrible idea — where the stakes are real, the chemistry is undeniable, and the happy ending is earned through fire and sacrifice, not convenience.

She writes surrounded by too many candles, has strong opinions about fictional fae kings, and has never met a forbidden love trope she didn't want to explore. Her books come with a warning: they will consume you.

## Long Bio (400 words)

Sera Nightingale has been in love with fairy tales since before she could read them herself — and suspicious of their endings for nearly as long. The originals, she noticed, were darker. The bargains cost more. The fae were not kind, and love between worlds was never simple. That suspicion became her writing philosophy: paranormal romance should feel genuinely perilous, and the happy ending should be earned through sacrifice, not handed out as a genre requirement.

Her heroines are not passive participants in their own stories. They negotiate, they fight back, they make terrible bargains with full knowledge of the price. When they fall in love, they do so with their eyes open — aware that the immortal beings they're drawn to are not human, do not think like humans, and may have agendas that span centuries. The tension in Sera's books comes not from misunderstanding but from genuine conflict: two people who want each other but whose worlds, values, or very natures make that wanting dangerous.

The fae courts in her novels are richly imagined political landscapes — hierarchical, ritualistic, and governed by rules that make human law seem quaint. Her worldbuilding draws from Celtic mythology, Arthurian legend, and the darker corners of European folklore, blended with her own innovations. The magic in her books is wild and costly, the immortals are genuinely alien in their thinking, and the mortal world exists in deliberate tension with the worlds beyond the veil.

Sera's slow burns are legendary among her readers. She believes romantic tension should build like pressure — scene by scene, glance by glance, until the release feels inevitable and explosive. She writes for readers who want to feel the yearning, who savor the almost-moments, and who understand that the best love stories are the ones that make you ache before they make you swoon.

She writes from a cluttered desk surrounded by candles that shouldn't be left unattended, owns more velvet than any reasonable person should, and maintains that morally ambiguous fae kings are simply better love interests than nice ones. Her books are for readers who want their romance with danger, their fantasy with heart, and their happy endings earned in blood and starlight.

## Protagonist Archetype
