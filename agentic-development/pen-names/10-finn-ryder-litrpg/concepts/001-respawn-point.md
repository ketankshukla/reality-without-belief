# Respawn Point

## Author
**Finn Ryder** — LitRPG

---

## Genre
LitRPG / GameLit / Progression Fantasy

---

## Concept

When Marcus Chen dies in the real world — hit by a truck while crossing the street — he expects oblivion. Instead, he wakes up at a respawn point. Not in a game. In actual reality. The world has changed while he wasn't looking: at some point in the past decade, Earth developed game-like mechanics. Levels, skills, stats — all real. Most people have been grinding their whole lives. Marcus is level one.

The respawn system has rules. You get three deaths before it's permanent. Marcus has already used one. He has two lives left to figure out how to survive in a world where the average person is level 30, monsters roam outside city safe zones, and the economy is based entirely on the System's mechanics.

Fortunately, Marcus was a professional gamer in his previous life. He knows how to optimize. He knows how to find exploits. And he knows that the most dangerous players aren't the high-levels — they're the ones who understand the System better than anyone else.

He has two respawns, zero skills, and the knowledge that somewhere in this world is an explanation for why he missed the awakening. Why he was left out while everyone else leveled up. And whether the people who did this to him are still watching.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (12 chapters)

2. **The New World** — Understanding what Earth became
3. **Level One** — Assessing his position
4. **The Grind Begins** — First skill gains
5. **The Meta** — Applying gaming knowledge
6. **The Gap** — How far behind he is
7. **The Exploit** — Finding an optimization
8. **Leveling Fast** — Rapid progression
9. **The Mystery** — Why was he left out?
10. **The Watchers** — Someone knows about him
11. **Second Death** — Using another respawn
12. **Respawn Point** — Resolution, new chapter begins
