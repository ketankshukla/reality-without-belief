## Concept

In Aethoria Online, combat classes get all the glory. Warriors, mages, assassins — they're the ones who clear dungeons, defeat bosses, and earn legendary rewards. Crafters are support. Background. The people who make weapons for real players to use.

Kai Chen chose Artificer anyway. He's not interested in swinging swords or casting fireballs. He wants to understand the crafting system — really understand it, the way no one else has bothered to. While other players grind combat levels, Kai grinds crafting theory.

What he discovers changes everything: the crafting system isn't separate from the combat system. It's foundational. The weapons and armor that warriors depend on, the potions that keep mages alive — they're all governed by rules that Kai can manipulate. Rules that combat classes can't even see.

When Kai creates a dagger that scales with Artificer level instead of Warrior level, he breaks the meta. When he crafts armor that improves based on crafting skill, he makes himself dangerous. And when he figures out how to create items that give crafting bonuses to combat actions, he becomes the most powerful player in the game — without ever swinging a weapon himself.

The combat guilds don't like having their power structure threatened by a crafter.
