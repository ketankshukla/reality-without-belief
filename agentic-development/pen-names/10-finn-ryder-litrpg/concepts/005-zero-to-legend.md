## Concept

When the System arrived, it evaluated everyone on Earth and assigned starting stats based on their "potential." Athletes got high Strength. Scientists got high Intelligence. Wealthy, successful people got bonuses across the board.

Jake Miller got zeroes. Literally. The System evaluated his potential and assigned him 0 in every stat. A glitch, the administrators said. A bug. Unfortunate but irreversible.

In a world where stats determine survival, Jake should be dead. Zero Strength means he can't lift a weapon. Zero Vitality means a single hit kills him. Zero Intelligence means he can't use skills that require mental stats. He is, by the System's measure, worthless.

But Jake notices something the System didn't account for: percentage-based effects. A skill that increases Strength by 50% does nothing when Strength is 10. But certain items and abilities don't use percentages — they add flat values. They're considered worthless by high-stat players. For Jake, they're everything.

He builds a character made entirely of flat bonuses, workarounds, and exploits. Every point of strength is precious. Every stat gain is a victory. And when he finally faces a boss that's killed high-stat parties, he wins — because he's spent hundreds of hours optimizing a build that others would never consider.
