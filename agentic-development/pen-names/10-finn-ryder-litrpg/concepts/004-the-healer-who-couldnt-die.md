## Concept

In most games, healers are squishy. High healing output, low survivability. Stand in the back, keep the party alive, die first if anything goes wrong. That's the conventional wisdom.

Sera Vance doesn't play conventional builds.

When the System awakens and Earth becomes a death game, Sera is a nurse — a healer by profession and instinct. The System gives her healing abilities, exactly as expected. What it doesn't expect is for her to spec entirely into self-healing and damage mitigation.

Every point goes into keeping herself alive. Passive regeneration. Damage reduction. Health conversion. Healing overflow that becomes temporary HP. By level 20, she can take hits that would kill warriors. By level 40, she's functionally immortal.

The problem is, she can't actually kill anything. Her offensive output is zero. She survives forever but can't progress alone. So she becomes something else entirely: a tank that never dies, a support that never falls, the backbone of every party she joins.

When the Apocalypse Dungeon opens — an endgame challenge that's killed every party that's attempted it — the raid leaders come looking for the healer who can't be killed. Even if it takes a thousand attempts, she'll survive long enough to figure out the mechanics.
