## Concept

Alex was a dungeon master — the tabletop kind, not the magical kind. He spent twenty years crafting adventures, designing encounters, balancing challenge and reward. Then he died, and the System offered him a job: become an actual dungeon.

As a Dungeon Core, Alex is a sapient crystal buried in the earth, capable of creating rooms, spawning monsters, placing traps, and luring adventurers to their doom. The more adventurers he kills, the more resources he gains to expand. The more he expands, the more powerful adventurers come. It's a progression system built on murder.

Alex has a problem: he doesn't want to kill people. His entire philosophy as a DM was that the best adventures let players succeed through cleverness and teamwork. He designed challenges, not death traps. Now the System expects him to be a meat grinder.

So Alex decides to do something no dungeon has ever done: he's going to create a dungeon that adventurers can actually clear. A fair dungeon. A training ground instead of a killing field. The System doesn't like this. Other dungeons consider him a traitor. And the adventurers? They don't trust a dungeon that doesn't want them dead.
