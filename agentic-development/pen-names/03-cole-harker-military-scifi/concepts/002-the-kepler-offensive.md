# The Kepler Offensive

## Author
**Cole Harker** — Military Sci-Fi

---

## Genre
Military Science Fiction / Space Combat / Tactical Thriller

---

## Concept

Admiral Yuki Tanaka has forty ships. The enemy has four hundred. She has six hours until they arrive at Kepler Station, where two million civilians are waiting for evacuation transports that won't arrive in time.

There is no winning this battle. Tanaka knows it. Her officers know it. High Command knows it, which is why they've already written off Kepler Station in their strategic planning — they just haven't told anyone.

Tanaka refuses to accept the math.

Her tactical officer, Commander David Chen, has been running simulations for three days. Every scenario ends the same way: total destruction within ninety minutes of engagement. But buried in the data is an anomaly — a pattern in enemy fleet movements suggesting their command-and-control is more centralized than doctrine assumes.

If they can identify and destroy the command ship, the enemy fleet might fragment. Long enough for evacuation. Long enough for some of Tanaka's ships to survive. Maybe.

The problem is, they can't identify the command ship until the battle begins. And every minute spent searching is a minute the enemy spends destroying her fleet.

This is a book about six hours. About the decisions made when time is the enemy. About forty crews who know they're probably going to die and fight anyway, because there are two million people behind them who can't.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (10 chapters)

1. **T-Minus 6 Hours** — The situation, the impossible math
2. **T-Minus 5 Hours** — Planning, the command ship theory
3. **T-Minus 4 Hours** — Fleet preparation, crew moments
4. **T-Minus 3 Hours** — Enemy detected, final positioning
5. **T-Minus 2 Hours** — Approach, tension building
6. **T-Minus 1 Hour** — Pre-battle briefings, goodbyes
7. **Contact** — Battle begins, chaos and casualties
8. **The Search** — Looking for command ship under fire
9. **The Strike** — Identifying and attacking the target
10. **The Kepler Offensive** — Resolution, who survives
