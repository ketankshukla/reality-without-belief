# The Titan Accord

## Author
**Cole Harker** — Military Sci-Fi

---

## Genre
Military Science Fiction / Political Thriller / War Fiction

---

## Concept

The Titan Accord ended thirty years of war. Two hours after the signing, someone fired on a civilian transport in the demilitarized zone. Three hundred dead, including the children of diplomats from both sides.

Everyone is pointing at someone else.

Captain Yara Singh commands the investigation vessel sent to determine who fired the shot. She has representatives from both governments aboard, a crew split between former enemies, and seventy-two hours before the ceasefire collapses into resumed hostilities. Both sides want answers. Neither side wants the truth if it implicates them.

The evidence is contradictory. The weapon signatures don't match any known fleet. The trajectory analysis suggests impossible firing positions. And someone on Singh's own ship is actively sabotaging the investigation.

As Singh digs deeper, she realizes this wasn't a mistake or a rogue actor. The attack was precision-engineered to restart the war — by parties on both sides who profit from continued conflict. The Titan Accord threatened too many interests. Someone decided peace was unacceptable.

Singh has the evidence. She also has enemies on her own bridge, a deadline that's already passed, and the knowledge that revealing the truth might cause as much damage as the lie.

## Key Themes
- Peace as threat to those who profit from war
- Investigation under impossible pressure
- Trust between former enemies
- Truth vs. stability

## Tone
Political thriller with military SF backbone. Clock ticking, loyalties uncertain.

---

## Unique Angle
Murder mystery in the DMZ. The crime is designed to restart a war.

---

## Target Audience
Readers who love political military SF, investigations under pressure, morally complex scenarios.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (10 chapters)

1. **The Accord** — Ceasefire signed, the attack
2. **The Assignment** — Singh's mission, the mixed crew
3. **Evidence** — Initial investigation, contradictions
4. **Sabotage** — Someone interfering
5. **Theories** — What the attack was designed to do
6. **Deeper** — Finding the conspiracy
7. **Countdown** — Ceasefire collapsing
8. **The Truth** — Who's really responsible
9. **The Choice** — What to do with the evidence
10. **The Titan Accord** — Resolution
