# Battle Group Obsidian

## Author
**Cole Harker** — Military Sci-Fi

---

## Genre
Military Science Fiction / Survival / War Fiction

---

## Concept

Battle Group Obsidian was cut off behind enemy lines six months ago. High Command declared them lost in action. The memorial services have been held. Their families have grieved.

But Obsidian isn't dead. They're trapped.

Commander Sara Vance leads what's left of her battle group — three cruisers, two destroyers, and a carrier with no fighters left — through enemy-controlled space. They survive by running, by hiding in asteroid fields and gas giant atmospheres, by scavenging supplies from the wreckage of battles they weren't part of.

For six months, the goal has been simple: survive long enough to find a way home. But survival isn't sustainable. Supplies are failing. Crew morale is cracking. And the enemy has learned that Obsidian exists — a ghost fleet that's been raiding their supply lines, and they want it destroyed.

Vance faces a choice: keep running until they're inevitably caught, or go on the offensive. Stop fighting to survive and start fighting to win. Turn a fleet that's been written off into something the enemy fears.

The problem is, attacking means accepting that they might not make it home. It means trading the hope of survival for the certainty of impact. But if they're going to die behind enemy lines, Vance wants to die doing damage.

---

## Key Themes
- Survival vs. purpose when both seem impossible
- Leadership in isolation
- What a "lost" fleet owes to those who've written them off
- Fighting to matter, not just to live

---

## Tone
Desperate, determined, finding hope in aggression rather than defense.

---

## Unique Angle
The fleet behind enemy lines story from the perspective of those who've already been mourned.

---

## Target Audience
