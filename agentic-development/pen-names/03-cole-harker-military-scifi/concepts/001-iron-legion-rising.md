# Iron Legion Rising

## Author
**Cole Harker** — Military Sci-Fi

---

## Genre
Military Science Fiction / Space Opera / War Fiction

---

## Concept

Admiral Vera Chen was the hero of the Proxima Campaign — until the court martial. They said she sacrificed three destroyers unnecessarily, that she'd become reckless after the death of her daughter in the Kepler Massacre. They stripped her rank, gave her a medical discharge, and buried her service record.

Five years later, the Hegemony is losing the war.

The enemy, a coalition of breakaway colonies calling themselves the Free Systems Alliance, has learned to counter every tactic in the fleet's playbook. Morale is collapsing. Strategic positions are falling. And someone in High Command has finally done the math: Chen's "unnecessary sacrifice" at Proxima bought time for the evacuation of three million civilians. She wasn't reckless. She was the only one willing to make hard calls.

They reinstate her with a new command: the 47th Battle Group, nicknamed "the Iron Legion" — a fleet of damaged ships, problem officers, and crew that other commanders have written off. Her mission: hold the Kessler Line, a string of defensive stations protecting the core worlds.

The catch: she has seventeen ships against an enemy fleet of over two hundred. And High Command has made it clear that reinforcements aren't coming.

Chen doesn't need reinforcements. She needs her crew to stop seeing themselves as expendable and start seeing themselves as dangerous. She needs to turn the Iron Legion into something the enemy has never faced: a fleet that fights like it has nothing left to lose, because it doesn't.

---

## Key Themes
- Redemption through impossible command
- Making broken things dangerous
- The gap between political decisions and battlefield reality
- Leadership when hope is a luxury

---

## Tone
Tactical, character-driven, with the weight of command in every decision.

---

## Unique Angle
The "reject fleet" trope with genuine tactical depth. Chen wins through leadership and strategy, not miraculous technology.

---

## Target Audience
Readers of David Weber, Jack Campbell, Marko Kloos who want character-focused military SF.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (12 chapters)

1. **The Recall** — Chen learning she's been reinstated
2. **The Iron Legion** — Meeting her new command, assessing the damage
3. **The Line** — Understanding the Kessler defensive position
4. **First Blood** — Initial skirmish, demonstrating current problems
5. **Breaking Them Down** — Dealing with problem officers
6. **Building Them Up** — Training, trust-building
7. **The Enemy Arrives** — Main Alliance fleet approaches
8. **First Defense** — Holding the line, costly but successful
9. **The Price** — Losses mounting, morale tested
10. **Adaptation** — Changing tactics, surprising the enemy
11. **The Breaking Point** — Everything falling apart
12. **Iron Legion Rising** — Final stand, the fleet reborn
