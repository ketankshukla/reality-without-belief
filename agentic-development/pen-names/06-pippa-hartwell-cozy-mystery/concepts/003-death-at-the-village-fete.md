# Death at the Village Fête

## Author
**Pippa Hartwell** — Cozy Mystery

---

## Genre
Cozy Mystery / Amateur Sleuth / Village Celebration Mystery

---

## Concept

The annual Thornbury Village Fête is the highlight of the social calendar — flower competitions, baking contests, the crowning of the Fête Queen, and this year, a murder.

Margaret "Maggie" Chen, retired history teacher and current president of the Thornbury Historical Society, finds herself investigating when the body of Victor Ashworth is discovered behind the jam-tasting tent. Victor was the most hated man in the village: a property developer who'd been buying up cottages, evicting longtime residents, and planning to turn the village green into a shopping complex.

Half the village wanted him dead. The other half wanted him worse than dead. But wanting and doing are different things, and Maggie knows that only one person actually struck the fatal blow.

The fête continues around her — the village insisting that Victor wouldn't have wanted them to cancel (he would have, but nobody cares). Between judging the Victoria sponge competition and mediating the flower arrangement controversy, Maggie pieces together Victor's final hours. The clues are scattered across the fête grounds, hidden in the very competitions Victor tried to shut down.

1. **The Fête Begins** — Annual celebration, tensions
2. **The Body** — Victor found dead
3. **The Investigation Starts** — Police arrive, Maggie's curiosity
4. **The Suspects** — Everyone had motive
5. **The Competitions** — Clues in the contests
6. **The Property Schemes** — What Victor was really planning
7. **Village Secrets** — Long-held grudges
8. **The Pattern** — Connecting the clues
9. **The Confrontation** — Maggie corners the killer
10. **The Fête Queen** — Celebration continues, justice done
