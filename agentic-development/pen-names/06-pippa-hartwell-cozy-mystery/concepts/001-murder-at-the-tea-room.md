# Murder at the Tea Room

## Author
**Pippa Hartwell** — Cozy Mystery

---

## Genre
Cozy Mystery / Amateur Sleuth / Village Mystery

---

## Concept

When Beatrice "Bea" Thornton retired from her career as a forensic accountant in London, she planned to spend her golden years running a quiet tea room in the Cotswolds village of Little Wittering. The Lavender & Thyme Tea Room was supposed to be her peaceful finale — scones, clotted cream, and absolutely no numbers.

Then Mrs. Harriet Pembrooke, the village's most unpleasant resident, is found face-down in Bea's signature Earl Grey lavender blend, quite dead.

The local police constable, who's more accustomed to lost sheep than murder investigations, is convinced it's a tragic accident. But Bea notices things the police don't: the teacup positioned wrong for a right-handed woman, the peculiar timing of Mrs. Pembrooke's visit, and the fact that at least six people in Little Wittering had excellent reasons to want her dead.

Unable to let the inconsistencies go, Bea begins her own investigation — much to the annoyance of Detective Inspector Simon Ashford from the county police, who arrives to take over the case and immediately clashes with the meddling tea room owner.

Between serving customers and dodging the detective's irritation, Bea unravels the village's secrets: affairs, financial improprieties, and grudges held for decades. The murderer is someone she's served tea to. The question is which one.

## Target Audience
Cozy mystery readers, fans of M.C. Beaton, Richard Osman, those who love village settings.

## Suggested Chapter Structure (12 chapters)

1. **The Tea Room** — Bea's new life in Little Wittering
2. **The Body** — Mrs. Pembrooke found dead
3. **The Investigation Begins** — Police dismissal, Bea's doubts
4. **The Detective** — DI Ashford arrives
5. **The Suspects** — Six people with motive
6. **The Secrets** — Village gossip and grudges
7. **The Numbers** — Bea finds financial irregularities
8. **Narrowing Down** — Eliminating suspects
9. **The Confrontation** — Bea in danger
10. **The Truth** — The killer revealed
11. **Justice Served** — Resolution
12. **Back to Tea** — Peace restored
