# The Dying God's War

## Author
**Kael Varen** — Grimdark Fantasy

---

## Genre
Dark Fantasy / Grimdark / Divine War

---

## Concept

When a god falls from the heavens, it doesn't just die. It takes three days to bleed out, and in those three days, anyone who reaches its corpse can drink its blood and claim a fraction of divine power. 

The god Vashren has fallen in the neutral territory between three warring nations. Each nation has dispatched its champion: Torren the Unbroken, warrior-king of the Ironlands; Saya the Whisper, spymaster of the Veiled Court; and Brennen the Last, sole survivor of a monastic order that worshipped Vashren itself. Each believes they deserve the god's power. Each has the strength to take it.

But Vashren isn't dead yet. The dying god is still conscious, still aware, and it's watching the mortals race toward its corpse with something between amusement and rage. It didn't fall by accident. It was pushed. And it knows who pushed it.

As the three champions converge, they discover they're not competing for power — they're being gathered. Vashren has enough strength left for one final act: it can choose who receives its power, and it will choose the one who agrees to hunt down and destroy whoever killed it. The war isn't for divine inheritance. It's an audition for divine vengeance.

---

## Key Themes
- What gods owe mortals (and vice versa)
- The cost of divine power
- Vengeance as inheritance
- Whether mortal champions can trust dying deities

---

## Tone
Epic in scale, intimate in execution. Three perspectives on the same dying god.

---

## Unique Angle
The god is still alive, still choosing, still manipulating. The race isn't about strength — it's about who'll accept the most terrible bargain.

---

## Target Audience
Readers who love divine politics, morally compromised champions, and epic fantasy that's actually about the gods.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (12 chapters)

1. **The Fall** — Vashren hits the earth; three nations mobilize
2. **The Ironlands Champion** — Torren's perspective, his reasons
3. **The Veiled Court's Champion** — Saya's perspective, her agenda
4. **The Last Worshipper** — Brennen's perspective, his grief
5. **Convergence** — Three paths toward one corpse
6. **First Blood** — Initial conflicts, alliances considered
7. **The God Speaks** — Vashren reveals itself still conscious
8. **The Bargain Offered** — What the god wants in exchange for power
9. **Refusals and Acceptances** — Each champion's response
10. **The Murder** — What happened in heaven; who pushed Vashren
11. **The Choice** — Who drinks the blood
12. **The Dying God's War** — The hunt begins
