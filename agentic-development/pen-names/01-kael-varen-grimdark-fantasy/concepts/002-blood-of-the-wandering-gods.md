# Blood of the Wandering Gods

## Author
**Kael Varen** — Grimdark Fantasy

---

## Genre
Dark Fantasy / Grimdark / Sword and Sorcery

---

## Concept

The gods are dead. Everyone knows this. The Wandering Gods — seven deities who walked the earth in flesh — were hunted down and killed three centuries ago by the mortal armies they'd once protected. Their blood soaked into the land, and from that blood grew the god-metals: materials of impossible power that fuel the current age's magical weapons and armor.

Torvin is a Bloodhunter — one of the professional scavengers who dig through ancient battlefields searching for fragments of god-metal. It's filthy, dangerous work in cursed places, but it pays. Then his crew unearths something that shouldn't exist: a god's heart, still beating, three hundred years after death.

Within days, everyone who knows about the heart starts dying. Someone very powerful wants it suppressed. Torvin barely escapes with his life and the heart itself — a relic that whispers to him, offering power, offering knowledge, offering the chance to resurrect the dead god in exchange for services he doesn't understand.

The thing is, the gods weren't good. They protected humanity the way farmers protect livestock — for use. The Mortal Rebellion that killed them was brutal but justified. Resurrecting a god might doom the world to a new age of divine tyranny. But the forces hunting Torvin leave him no choice: the only way to survive is to accept the dead god's bargain.

As Torvin runs, he discovers that not all the gods died. Some are in hiding. Some are waiting. And some are very interested in a mortal who carries their fallen brother's heart.

---

## Key Themes
- The morality of divine rule (even benevolent tyranny is tyranny)
- What do mortals owe the gods who made them?
- The corrupting influence of power offered freely
- Legacy and the lies we tell about history
- Whether some powers should stay dead

---

## Tone
Gritty, cynical, with moments of awe at the scale of divine power. Torvin is a survivor, not a hero — his morality is flexible but not absent. The world feels old and bloodstained.

---

## Unique Angle
The gods aren't coming back — one of them is being *resurrected through a protagonist who doesn't want to do it but has no other option*. The divine is a threat, not a salvation.

---

## Target Audience
Readers of Mark Lawrence, Brian McClellan, Michael R. Fletcher. Those who like their fantasy with complicated divinity and morally flexible protagonists.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (11 chapters)

1. **The Dig** — Torvin's crew excavating god-metal; the discovery of the heart
2. **The First Death** — Crew members start dying; Torvin realizes someone knows
3. **Flight** — Escape with the heart; the whispers begin
4. **The Offer** — The dead god's bargain made explicit; what it wants
5. **Hunted** — Professional killers on his trail; he learns who sent them
6. **The First Bargain** — Torvin accepts limited help, pays a price
7. **The Hidden God** — Encounter with a surviving deity; revelations about the war
8. **The True History** — What really happened when the gods died, and why
9. **The Second Bargain** — Deeper power, deeper cost
10. **Convergence** — Multiple parties close in; Torvin must choose
11. **Blood of Gods** — Final confrontation; the resurrection completed or denied
