# Wolves of the Shattered Kingdom

## Author
**Kael Varen** — Grimdark Fantasy

---

## Genre
Dark Fantasy / Grimdark / Mercenary Fiction

---

## Concept

They called themselves the Silver Wolves when they were the elite guard of King Aldric the Just. Now they call themselves nothing — just eight survivors of a company of three hundred, living in the ruins of a kingdom that no longer exists.

When the Pale Conquest came, the Silver Wolves held the capital's gates for nine days while the kingdom collapsed behind them. They fought for evacuation routes that civilians used to flee. They bought time for a retreat that never came. And when they finally broke through enemy lines, they discovered that King Aldric had already surrendered, sold out his own people for a comfortable exile, and declared the Silver Wolves traitors for continuing to fight.

Now they're outlaws in the land they defended. The Pale Conquerors have put bounties on their heads. The nobles who survived the conquest pretend they never existed. The common people are too terrified to shelter them. They survive by stealing from their former enemies and selling their swords to anyone who'll pay — anyone except the Pale.

But when intelligence reaches them that King Aldric, living fat in exile, is negotiating to betray the last resistance cells in exchange for land grants, the Wolves face a choice: let the resistance die and stay safe, or cross enemy lines to assassinate the king they once swore to protect.

It's not revenge. It's not justice. It's just the last job that matters.

---

## Key Themes
- Loyalty betrayed and what remains after
- The bond of shared survival
- What oaths mean when the person you swore them to is worthless
- Finding meaning after everything you fought for is gone
- Whether some kills are more about closure than consequence

---

## Tone
Bitter, brotherhood-forged, with dark humor born of shared trauma. The Wolves bicker like family and kill like professionals.

---

## Unique Angle
A mercenary band story where the mercenaries didn't start as mercenaries — they were idealists broken by betrayal, and their final mission is to kill the man they once would have died for.

---

## Target Audience
Readers who love *The Black Company*, *The Blade Itself*, military fantasy with complicated loyalties.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (11 chapters)

1. **What Remains** — The eight survivors, their current existence
2. **The Silver Days** — Flashback to what they were, who they served
3. **The Nine Days** — The fall of the capital, condensed
4. **The Betrayal** — Learning of the king's surrender and their outlawing
5. **Wolves for Hire** — Current jobs, current compromises
6. **The Intelligence** — News of Aldric's new betrayal; the decision
7. **Into Enemy Territory** — Crossing into the Pale's lands
8. **The Resistance Contact** — Alliance with those who still fight
9. **The King in Exile** — Reaching Aldric, seeing what he's become
10. **The Hunt** — Closing in while Pale forces close in on them
11. **Last Wolves** — The assassination; what comes after
