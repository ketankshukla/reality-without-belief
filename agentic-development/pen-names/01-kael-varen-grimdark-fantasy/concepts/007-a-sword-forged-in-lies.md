# A Sword Forged in Lies

## Author
**Kael Varen** — Grimdark Fantasy

---

## Genre
Dark Fantasy / Grimdark / Time-Bent Assassin Fantasy

---

## Concept

Lira Vex is the Shadow Court's most reliable assassin — until she opens a contract scroll and sees her own face looking back at her. Not her face now, at thirty-two, but her face at seventeen: the girl she was before the Shadow Court found her, before she became a weapon.

The contract is authentic. The payment has already been made. Someone wants that seventeen-year-old dead, and the magic binding the contract means Lira must complete it — or die herself. The Shadow Court's contracts are absolute.

But this isn't a simple matter of time travel. The girl exists now, in this timeline — a version of Lira from a parallel possibility, one where she never became an assassin. This Lira grew up loved. This Lira became a healer. This Lira has a family, a community, a life that the assassin Lira can barely comprehend.

Killing her should be easy. She's soft, trusting, unprepared. But the more Lira shadows her target, the more she sees the life she could have had — and the more she wonders who would benefit from erasing that possibility. The contract's origin is hidden, but the implications are clear: someone knows about parallel selves, someone is weaponizing that knowledge, and Lira isn't the only assassin who's been given themselves as a target.

To survive, she'll have to break the one rule the Shadow Court has: she'll have to refuse a contract.

## Key Themes
- The selves we could have been
- Whether redemption is possible for the irredeemable
- What we owe alternate versions of ourselves
- Breaking systems that bind us

---

## Tone
Noir-inflected, identity-haunted. Lira is professionally detached until she can't be.

---

## Unique Angle
Self-as-target with parallel universe mechanics. The assassin must kill who she could have been.

---

## Target Audience
Readers who like identity-focused fantasy, parallel universe concepts, assassin protagonists facing impossible choices.

---

## Suggested Chapter Structure (10 chapters)

1. **The Contract** — Receiving the impossible assignment
2. **The Rules** — Why she can't refuse
3. **The Other** — First observation of her alternate self
4. **The Life** — What the other Lira has; what was possible
5. **The Shadow** — Getting closer, building cover
6. **The Connection** — Accidental contact; unexpected resonance
7. **The Pattern** — Discovering other assassins with similar contracts
8. **The Origin** — Who benefits from eliminating possibilities
9. **The Refusal** — Breaking the contract's binding
10. **A Sword Forged in Lies** — Confronting whoever's behind this
